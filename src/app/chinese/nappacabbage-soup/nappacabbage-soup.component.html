Chinese-Style Stir-Fried Napa Cabbage Recipe

Napa Cabbage (also known as Chinese cabbage, 大白菜) is a versatile vegetable often used in Chinese cuisine for stir-fries, soups, or dumpling fillings. Here’s a simple and delicious recipe for Chinese-Style Stir-Fried Napa Cabbage that highlights the vegetable’s natural sweetness and tender-crisp texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium napa cabbage (about 500g)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2-3 slices of ginger
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 cup water or vegetable stock
  • 1/2 tsp sesame oil (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper (or black pepper)
  • 1-2 red chilies, sliced (optional, for heat)
  • Chopped green onions for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Napa Cabbage

Cut the cabbage: Remove any damaged outer leaves of the napa cabbage. Cut the cabbage lengthwise into quarters, then slice into 2-3 inch pieces, separating the white stems from the leafy green tops. The stems need a little more time to cook than the leaves.

Rinse and drain: Wash the napa cabbage thoroughly under cold water and drain well.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce

Mix the sauce: In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, and water or vegetable stock. Set aside.

Step 3: Heat the Oil

Heat the wok: In a large wok or skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.

Add garlic and ginger: Add the minced garlic and ginger slices to the wok, stir-frying for about 30 seconds to 1 minute until fragrant but not browned.

Step 4: Stir-Fry the Napa Cabbage Stems

Cook the white stems first: Add the thicker white stems of the napa cabbage to the wok first, as they take longer to cook than the leafy tops. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the stems are starting to soften but still have a bit of crunch.

Step 5: Add the Leafy Green Tops

Add the leaves: Now add the leafy green tops of the napa cabbage to the wok. Stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes until the leaves are just wilted but still vibrant in color.

Pour in the sauce: Pour the prepared soy sauce mixture over the cabbage and stir well to coat all the pieces evenly.

Step 6: Simmer and Finish

Simmer: Let the cabbage cook in the sauce for another 1-2 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the cabbage to soften to your preferred texture.

Add finishing touches: Drizzle with a little sesame oil and sprinkle white pepper for extra flavor. If you like heat, toss in the sliced red chilies at this stage.

Step 7: Serve

Serve immediately: Transfer the stir-fried napa cabbage to a serving dish. Garnish with chopped green onions, if desired, and serve hot as a side dish with rice or as part of a larger Chinese meal.

Tips for Success:

  • Crisp texture: Avoid overcooking the napa cabbage to keep the white stems slightly crisp while the leaves are tender.
  • Balance the flavors: Adjust the seasoning to your taste—add a little more soy sauce for saltiness, or sugar to balance the dish's flavors.
  • Protein add-ins: You can add sliced mushrooms, tofu, or small pieces of meat like pork or chicken to make this dish more filling.

This Chinese-Style Stir-Fried Napa Cabbage is quick, nutritious, and flavorful, making it an excellent side dish for any meal. Enjoy the tender crunch of the napa cabbage paired with savory and aromatic seasonings!
