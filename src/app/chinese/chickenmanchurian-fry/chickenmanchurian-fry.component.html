Chicken Manchurian Recipe

Chicken Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish that features crispy chicken pieces coated in a tangy, savory, and slightly spicy sauce. It’s a fusion of Chinese flavors adapted to Indian tastes. Here’s an easy recipe to make Chicken Manchurian in Chinese style.

Ingredients:

For the Chicken:

  • 500g (1 lb) boneless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Oil for frying

For the Manchurian Sauce:

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (optional for extra heat)
  • 1 small onion, chopped or cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup bell peppers (capsicum), cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar (white or rice vinegar)
  • 1 tbsp ketchup
  • 1 tbsp chili sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 1/4 cup water (for thickening)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp white or black pepper
  • 1/2 cup water or chicken broth
  • Spring onions (green onions) for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare and Fry the Chicken

Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with cornstarch, all-purpose flour, soy sauce, egg, salt, and black pepper. Let it marinate for 15-20 minutes.

Fry the chicken: Heat oil in a pan or deep fryer. Fry the chicken in batches until golden and crispy, about 4-5 minutes per batch. Remove and drain on paper towels.

Step 2: Prepare the Manchurian Sauce

Sauté aromatics: In a large wok or pan, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies, and sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.

Cook the vegetables: Add the chopped onions and bell peppers to the pan. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened but still crisp.

Step 3: Make the Sauce

Add the sauces: Stir in the soy sauce, vinegar, ketchup, chili sauce, sugar, and black or white pepper. Mix well to combine.

Thicken the sauce: Add the cornstarch slurry (1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 1/4 cup water) to the pan and stir continuously until the sauce thickens, about 1-2 minutes. If the sauce is too thick, add a little more water or broth to adjust the consistency.

Step 4: Combine Chicken with Sauce

Toss the chicken: Add the fried chicken pieces to the sauce and toss well to coat the chicken evenly. Let it simmer for 2-3 minutes to absorb the flavors.

Garnish: Sprinkle chopped spring onions on top.

Step 5: Serve

Serve hot: Chicken Manchurian is best served hot with steamed rice, fried rice, or noodles.

Tips for Success:

  • Crispy chicken: To keep the chicken crispy, fry it in batches and make sure the oil is hot enough.
  • Adjusting the spice level: If you prefer less heat, reduce or omit the green chilies and chili sauce. For extra heat, add more chili sauce or fresh chilies.
  • Balance the sauce: Adjust the seasoning of the sauce to your taste. You can make it tangier by adding more vinegar or sweeter by increasing the sugar.

This Chicken Manchurian combines crispy fried chicken with a tangy, savory sauce, making it a perfect dish to enjoy with rice or noodles for an Indo-Chinese feast!
