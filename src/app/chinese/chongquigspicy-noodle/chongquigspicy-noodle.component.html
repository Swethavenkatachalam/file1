Chongqing Spicy Noodles Recipe (重庆小面)

Chongqing Spicy Noodles (重庆小面) is a fiery noodle dish from Chongqing, China, known for its bold and spicy flavors, with a rich mix of chili oil, Sichuan peppercorns, and aromatic spices. This noodle dish is perfect for those who love heat and is often served with or without meat.

Ingredients:

For the Noodles:

  • 400g (14 oz) fresh or dried Chinese wheat noodles (or any thin noodles)

For the Sauce Base:

  • 2-3 tbsp chili oil with sediment (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns, ground
  • 1 tbsp Chinese black vinegar (or rice vinegar)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1-2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp doubanjiang (Sichuan broad bean chili paste)
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth or hot water (to adjust the sauce consistency)

For the Toppings:

  • 200g (7 oz) ground pork or beef (optional for a meat version)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce (for the meat)
  • 1 tsp Shaoxing wine (for the meat)
  • 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, crushed (for extra flavor with the meat)
  • Green onions, chopped
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)
  • Toasted peanuts or sesame seeds (optional)
  • Blanched leafy greens (like bok choy or spinach)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Sauce Base

Mix the sauce: In a large serving bowl, combine chili oil with sediment, ground Sichuan peppercorns, Chinese black vinegar, soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, minced garlic, doubanjiang, and Shaoxing wine. Stir well to create a smooth, spicy sauce base.

Adjust the consistency: Add the chicken broth or hot water to adjust the sauce to your desired consistency.

Step 2: Cook the Noodles

Boil the noodles: Bring a pot of water to a boil and cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Fresh noodles will take only 2-3 minutes, while dried noodles will take around 4-5 minutes. Drain the noodles and set aside.

Step 3: Cook the Meat (Optional)

Cook the ground pork or beef: Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the ground meat and cook until browned and crispy.

Add seasonings: Stir in soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and crushed Sichuan peppercorns, cooking for another 3-5 minutes until the meat is well seasoned. Set aside.

Step 4: Prepare the Toppings

Blanch the greens: Blanch the leafy greens (like bok choy or spinach) in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then drain and set aside.

Chop garnishes: Prepare the chopped green onions, cilantro, and toasted peanuts or sesame seeds for topping.

Step 5: Assemble the Chongqing Spicy Noodles

Add the noodles: Place the cooked noodles in the bowl with the sauce.

Top with meat: If using, spoon the cooked ground meat over the noodles.

Add vegetables: Place the blanched greens on top, along with any other garnishes like green onions, cilantro, toasted peanuts, or sesame seeds.

Step 6: Serve

Mix and enjoy: Before eating, toss the noodles well with the sauce and toppings to coat everything evenly. Adjust the seasoning with extra chili oil or vinegar, if desired.

Tips for Success:

  • Adjust spice level: You can control the heat by adjusting the amount of chili oil and doubanjiang. Add more or less according to your preference.
  • Noodle texture: Be sure to cook the noodles just until al dente and rinse with cold water if they need to wait a few minutes before serving, to prevent sticking.
  • Custom toppings: Feel free to customize the toppings by adding tofu, fried egg, or different types of vegetables.

Chongqing Spicy Noodles are all about bold, mouth-numbing flavors with the perfect balance of spicy, tangy, and savory notes. Enjoy this fiery Sichuan dish!
