Chinese-Style Wontons Recipe

Chinese-style Wontons are a traditional and comforting dish that can be enjoyed in soups, steamed, or fried. They are typically filled with a mixture of pork, shrimp, and seasonings, wrapped in thin wonton wrappers. Here’s an authentic recipe for Chinese Wontons, including a basic filling and cooking methods.

Ingredients:

For the Wonton Filling:

  • 200g (7 oz) ground pork
  • 100g (3.5 oz) shrimp, peeled and finely chopped
  • 2-3 stalks green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (Chinese cooking wine)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp ginger, finely minced or grated
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper (or black pepper)
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1/4 tsp salt (or to taste)

For Wrapping:

  • 1 pack of wonton wrappers (about 50 wrappers, store-bought or homemade)

For the Soup (optional):

  • 4-5 cups chicken or pork broth
  • 2-3 slices ginger
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Green onions and cilantro for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

Mix the filling: In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped shrimp, green onions, garlic, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, sesame oil, minced ginger, sugar, white pepper, cornstarch, and salt. Mix everything well until it becomes a smooth and slightly sticky filling. Let it marinate for about 15-20 minutes to enhance the flavors.

Step 2: Wrap the Wontons

Prepare the wrappers: Lay a wonton wrapper flat on a clean surface. Place about 1 teaspoon of the filling in the center of the wrapper.

Folding wontons: (Basic method):

  1. Moisten the edges of the wrapper with a little water (use your finger or a small brush).
  2. Fold the wrapper in half diagonally to form a triangle, pressing the edges together to seal.
  3. Bring the two opposite corners of the triangle together, moistening one corner and pressing them together to create the classic wonton shape.

Repeat this process until all the filling is used up.

Step 3: Cook the Wontons

Option 1: Boiling (For Soup)

Boil the wontons: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the wontons in batches (avoid overcrowding) and boil for 4-5 minutes, or until they float to the surface and are fully cooked. The filling should be firm, and the wrappers translucent.

Prepare the soup: While the wontons are boiling, heat the chicken or pork broth in another pot with ginger slices, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Let it simmer for 5-10 minutes to infuse the flavors.

Serve: Divide the cooked wontons into bowls and ladle hot broth over them. Garnish with chopped green onions and cilantro.

Option 2: Steaming

Steam the wontons: Place wontons in a steamer lined with parchment paper or lightly oiled. Steam over boiling water for about 8-10 minutes until the filling is cooked through. Serve with a dipping sauce made of soy sauce, rice vinegar, and chili oil.

Option 3: Frying

Fry the wontons: Heat oil in a deep pan or wok to about 350°F (175°C). Fry the wontons in batches for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy. Drain on a paper towel and serve hot with sweet chili sauce or soy dipping sauce.

Tips for Success:

  • Wonton Wrappers: Store-bought wonton wrappers work great, but be sure to keep them covered with a damp cloth while wrapping to prevent them from drying out.
  • Avoid Overfilling: Use about 1 teaspoon of filling per wonton to avoid tearing the wrappers.
  • Freezing Wontons: You can freeze uncooked wontons on a baking sheet and then transfer them to a freezer-safe bag. Boil or fry them directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.
  • Customizable Filling: You can adjust the filling by adding mushrooms, napa cabbage, or other vegetables to the meat mixture.

Enjoy your Chinese-Style Wontons in soup or as a snack, bursting with delicious flavors!
