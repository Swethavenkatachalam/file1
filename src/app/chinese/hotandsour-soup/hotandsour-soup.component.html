Hot and Sour Soup Recipe

Hot and Sour Soup is a classic Chinese dish, balancing spicy and tangy flavors with a rich broth. It's commonly made with ingredients like tofu, mushrooms, bamboo shoots, and pork or chicken, thickened with cornstarch and flavored with vinegar, white pepper, and chili oil. Ingredients:

For the Soup:

  • 6 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 100g (3.5 oz) pork loin or chicken breast, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 1 block (200g) firm tofu, cut into thin strips
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots, julienned
  • 1/2 cup shiitake mushrooms or wood ear mushrooms, soaked and sliced
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 2-3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar (or black vinegar)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (for thickening)
  • 1 tbsp chili oil (or more, to taste)
  • 1 tsp white pepper (or black pepper)
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped (for garnish)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish, optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Soak the mushrooms: If using dried shiitake or wood ear mushrooms, soak them in warm water for about 20-30 minutes until they soften. Slice thinly.

Julienne the bamboo shoots: Drain and thinly slice the bamboo shoots into matchstick-sized pieces.

Slice the tofu: Cut the tofu into thin strips for the soup.

Step 2: Cook the Pork (Optional)

Sear the pork or chicken: Heat a small amount of oil in a pot. Add the thinly sliced pork or chicken and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until just cooked. Remove and set aside.

Step 3: Prepare the Broth

Bring the broth to a boil: In a large pot, bring 6 cups of chicken or vegetable broth to a boil over medium heat.

Add the vegetables: Add the sliced mushrooms, bamboo shoots, and tofu strips to the pot. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes.

Step 4: Season the Soup

Soy sauce and vinegar: Add the soy sauce, rice vinegar (or black vinegar), sugar, and white pepper to the soup. Adjust the level of sourness by adding more vinegar to your taste.

Add the chili oil: Stir in chili oil for heat, adjusting the amount based on how spicy you prefer the soup.

Step 5: Thicken the Soup

Cornstarch slurry: Mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water until smooth. Slowly pour this mixture into the soup while stirring constantly to avoid clumping. Let the soup simmer for 1-2 minutes until slightly thickened.

Step 6: Add the Egg

Create the egg ribbons: While the soup is gently simmering, slowly drizzle the beaten egg into the soup in a thin stream. Stir the soup gently in one direction to create delicate egg ribbons.

Step 7: Finish and Serve

Sesame oil: Add a drizzle of sesame oil for extra flavor.

Garnish: Ladle the hot and sour soup into bowls and garnish with finely chopped green onions and fresh cilantro, if desired.

Tips for Success:

  • Adjust the flavors: The key to hot and sour soup is balancing the flavors. If it's too spicy, add more vinegar. If it's too sour, add a bit more soy sauce or sugar to mellow it out.
  • Texture: For a thicker soup, increase the cornstarch slurry. For a thinner soup, reduce or omit it.
  • Protein variations: You can add other proteins like shrimp or beef, or keep it vegetarian by skipping the meat entirely.
  • Vinegar: Rice vinegar gives a mild tang, but for a more traditional flavor, use Chinese black vinegar.

This Chinese-Style Hot and Sour Soup is a perfect combination of heat and tanginess with a hearty and flavorful broth. It's a comforting dish, ideal for cold days or when you're craving something warming and flavorful!
