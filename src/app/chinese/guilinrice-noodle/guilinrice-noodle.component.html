Guilin Rice Noodles Recipe (桂林米粉)

Guilin Rice Noodles (桂林米粉) is a famous dish from Guilin, China, known for its smooth rice noodles, savory broth, and flavorful toppings. This traditional dish can be customized with various ingredients like pork, pickled vegetables, and chili. Here's a recipe to help you create Guilin Rice Noodles in a Chinese style at home.

Ingredients:

For the Broth:

  • 500g (1 lb) pork bones (or beef bones)
  • 1 piece ginger (sliced)
  • 1 small onion (halved)
  • 2-3 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2-3 liters water

For the Noodles and Toppings:

  • 400g (14 oz) fresh or dried rice noodles
  • 200g (7 oz) roast pork or braised pork (thinly sliced)
  • 1/2 cup pickled vegetables (Chinese pickled mustard greens, zha cai, or other pickles)
  • 1-2 tbsp fried peanuts
  • 1-2 tbsp chopped green onions
  • Fresh cilantro (optional)
  • Chili oil (optional)
  • Soy sauce (for seasoning)
  • White pepper (for seasoning)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Broth

Blanch the bones: In a large pot, cover the pork bones with water and bring to a boil. Boil for 5-10 minutes to remove impurities. Drain the water and rinse the bones.

Simmer the broth: Return the bones to the pot and add 2-3 liters of fresh water. Add the ginger slices, onion, star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, Shaoxing wine, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for at least 2 hours to develop a rich flavor. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface.

Step 2: Cook the Rice Noodles

Prepare the noodles: If using dried rice noodles, soak them in warm water for 20-30 minutes until softened, then boil for 2-3 minutes. If using fresh noodles, boil them for just 1-2 minutes. Drain and rinse the noodles under cold water to stop the cooking process.
