Zha Jiang Mian Recipe (炸酱面)

Zha Jiang Mian (炸酱面) is a popular Chinese dish that translates to "fried sauce noodles." It's a comforting and flavorful dish made with thick wheat noodles and a savory meat sauce, typically pork, cooked with fermented soybean paste. Here's an authentic Zha Jiang Mian recipe in a Chinese style:

Ingredients:

For the Sauce:

  • 250g (9 oz) ground pork (or chicken)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 tbsp sweet bean sauce (Tian Mian Jiang, 甜面酱)
  • 2 tbsp fermented soybean paste (Doubanjiang, 豆瓣酱)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup water or chicken broth

For the Noodles:

  • 300g (10 oz) fresh or dried wheat noodles (or other thick noodles)

For the Toppings:

  • Cucumber, julienned
  • Carrot, julienned
  • Bean sprouts (blanched)
  • Green onions, thinly sliced
  • Fresh cilantro (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Cook the Meat Sauce

Heat the oil: In a wok or large pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.

Cook the pork: Add the ground pork and stir-fry until it turns golden brown and starts to get crispy, breaking it up into small pieces.

Add the sauces: Stir in the sweet bean sauce, fermented soybean paste (doubanjiang), soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and sugar. Stir well to coat the pork evenly in the sauce.

Simmer: Pour in the water or chicken broth and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens. Add sesame oil at the end for extra flavor. Set aside.

Step 2: Cook the Noodles

Boil the noodles: Bring a large pot of water to a boil and cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Fresh noodles will take a couple of minutes, while dried noodles may take 4-5 minutes. Drain the noodles and set aside.

Step 3: Prepare the Toppings

Julienne the vegetables: Thinly slice the cucumber and carrot into matchsticks. Blanch the bean sprouts briefly in boiling water and set aside.

Step 4: Assemble the Zha Jiang Mian

Divide the noodles: Place the cooked noodles in individual serving bowls.

Add the sauce: Spoon the meat sauce generously over the noodles.

Garnish: Add the cucumber, carrot, bean sprouts, green onions, and cilantro on top of the noodles.

Step 5: Serve

Mix and enjoy: To eat, mix the noodles with the sauce and toppings so everything is evenly coated. Serve immediately.

Tips for Success:

  • Adjust the sauce: Depending on the saltiness of the bean paste, you may need to adjust the amount of sugar or soy sauce to balance the flavor.
  • Texture variety: The crispy cucumber and carrots provide a nice contrast to the savory meat sauce, so don’t skip the fresh toppings.
  • Sauce consistency: If the sauce gets too thick, add a bit more water or broth to loosen it.

Enjoy your Zha Jiang Mian, a hearty, savory, and satisfying dish packed with authentic flavors from northern China!
