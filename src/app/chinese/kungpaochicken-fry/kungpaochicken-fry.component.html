Kung Pao Chicken Recipe

Kung Pao Chicken is a famous Sichuan-style dish known for its bold, spicy, and savory flavors, combined with a hint of sweetness. It features tender chicken stir-fried with peanuts, dried chili peppers, and a flavorful sauce. Here’s an authentic recipe for Chinese-Style Kung Pao Chicken:

Ingredients:

For the Chicken:

  • 500g (1 lb) boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Shaoxing wine (Chinese cooking wine)
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/4 tsp salt

For the Sauce:

  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar (or black vinegar)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce (optional for extra sweetness)

For Stir-Frying:

  • 3-4 dried red chili peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns (optional for authentic numbing spiciness)
  • 1 tbsp ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp garlic, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts (unsalted)
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, cut into small cubes
  • 2-3 green onions, chopped into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil (vegetable or peanut oil)

Garnish:

  • Chopped green onions
  • Sesame seeds (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

Prepare the marinade: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, cornstarch, sesame oil, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat. Let it marinate for 15-20 minutes to absorb the flavors.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce

Make the sauce: In a small bowl, mix the dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, cornstarch slurry, and hoisin sauce (if using). Stir until well combined and set aside.

Step 3: Fry the Peppers and Aromatics

Heat the oil: In a wok or large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat.

Fry the dried chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns: Add the dried red chilies and Sichuan peppercorns to the hot oil. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until they become fragrant, but be careful not to burn them. The peppers should darken slightly.

Sauté the aromatics: Add the chopped ginger and garlic. Stir-fry for another 30 seconds until fragrant.

Step 4: Cook the Chicken

Stir-fry the chicken: Add the marinated chicken to the wok and stir-fry over high heat for 3-4 minutes until the chicken is browned and cooked through.

Add the vegetables: Toss in the red and green bell peppers. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the peppers are slightly tender but still crisp.

Step 5: Add the Sauce and Peanuts

Pour in the sauce: Stir the sauce mixture and pour it over the chicken and vegetables. Toss everything together to coat evenly. Let it cook for another 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.

Add peanuts: Stir in the roasted peanuts and chopped green onions. Cook for another minute to blend the flavors.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Garnish: Sprinkle additional green onions or sesame seeds on top if desired.

Serve: Kung Pao Chicken is best served hot with steamed rice or fried rice.

Tips for Success:

  • Control the heat: Adjust the amount of dried chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns to your taste. If you prefer less heat, reduce the quantity or remove the seeds from the dried chilies.
  • Peanuts: Make sure to use unsalted, roasted peanuts for the best texture and flavor. You can toast them briefly in a dry pan if needed.
  • Cornstarch in the sauce: The cornstarch slurry helps thicken the sauce, giving it a glossy finish. Make sure to stir well while adding it to avoid clumps.

Enjoy this authentic Kung Pao Chicken, a perfect blend of spicy, savory, and slightly sweet flavors, packed with crunchy peanuts and tender chicken!
