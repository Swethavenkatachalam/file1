Chinese-Style Oxtail Soup Recipe

Chinese-Style Oxtail Soup is a comforting and flavorful dish, slow-cooked to allow the rich taste of oxtail to blend with the aromatics and herbs. The result is a hearty soup with tender meat and a deeply savory broth, commonly enjoyed in Chinese households. Here's an authentic recipe for Chinese-Style Oxtail Soup:

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 - 2 lbs (700-900g) oxtail, cut into sections
  • 8-10 cups water (or beef broth for a richer flavor)
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 large daikon radish, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 3-4 slices ginger
  • 3 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1-2 green onions, tied into a knot
  • 1-2 star anise
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 tbsp Shaoxing wine (Chinese cooking wine)
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce (for color, optional)
  • 1 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper (or black pepper)
  • Fresh cilantro or green onions for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Blanch the Oxtail

Parboil the oxtail: In a large pot, bring enough water to cover the oxtail to a boil. Add the oxtail and blanch for 5-7 minutes to remove impurities and excess fat.

Drain and rinse: Remove the oxtail from the water, drain, and rinse them under cold water. Set aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Aromatics and Vegetables

Chop the vegetables: Cut the carrot, daikon radish, and onion into large chunks.

Prepare the aromatics: Smash the garlic cloves and slice the ginger. Tie the green onions into a knot for easier removal later.

Step 3: Start the Soup

Sear the oxtail (optional): For extra depth of flavor, you can sear the oxtail in a little oil until browned on all sides in a large soup pot. This step is optional but adds more richness to the soup.

Simmer the oxtail: In the same pot, add 8-10 cups of water (or beef broth). Add the blanched oxtail, ginger, garlic, onion, green onion knot, star anise, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick.

Add seasonings: Pour in Shaoxing wine, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce (if using). Bring everything to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer gently.

Step 4: Simmer the Soup

Long, slow cooking: Cover the pot and let the soup simmer for 2-3 hours on low heat. Skim off any foam or fat that rises to the surface. The oxtail should become tender, and the broth should be infused with rich flavors.

Step 5: Add Vegetables

Add carrots and daikon: After 2-3 hours of simmering, add the carrot and daikon chunks to the pot. Continue to cook for another 30-40 minutes, or until the vegetables are soft and the oxtail is fall-off-the-bone tender.

Step 6: Season and Finish

Adjust seasoning: Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt and white pepper as needed.

Optional step: If you prefer a clearer broth, strain the soup and remove the aromatics like the ginger, garlic, star anise, cinnamon, and bay leaves.

Step 7: Serve

Serve hot: Ladle the soup into bowls, ensuring each serving has a generous portion of oxtail and vegetables. Garnish with chopped cilantro or green onions for a fresh touch.

Tips for Success:

  • Slow cooking: This recipe is best when cooked slowly over a low flame. The longer you simmer, the more flavorful the soup and tender the oxtail will become.
  • Skimming the fat: Oxtail can be fatty, so skimming off the excess fat during cooking helps to make a lighter broth. Alternatively, refrigerate the soup overnight and remove the solidified fat the next day.
  • Variations: You can add other vegetables like potatoes or tomatoes for extra flavor. You can also add a handful of goji berries or jujube dates for a sweet, herbal note often found in Chinese soups.

This Chinese-Style Oxtail Soup is comforting and nourishing, with tender oxtail and rich broth infused with warming spices and aromatics. Perfect for a cozy meal!
