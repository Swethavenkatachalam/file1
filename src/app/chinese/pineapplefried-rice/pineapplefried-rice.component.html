Pineapple Fried Rice Recipe

Pineapple Fried Rice is a delicious and refreshing dish often enjoyed in Chinese and Southeast Asian cuisine. Combining the sweetness of pineapple with savory ingredients like rice, vegetables, and sometimes meat or seafood, this dish is both flavorful and colorful. Ingredients:

For the Rice:

  • 3 cups cooked jasmine rice (day-old or cold rice works best)
  • 1 small pineapple (or 1 cup fresh pineapple chunks)
  • 2 eggs (lightly beaten)
  • 2-3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas and carrots (or other mixed vegetables)
  • 2-3 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup roasted cashews or peanuts (optional, for crunch)
  • 1-2 red chilies, sliced (optional, for heat)

For the Seasoning:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (optional for extra umami)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper (or black pepper)

Optional Add-ins:

  • 150g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 150g chicken breast, diced
  • 1/2 cup ham, diced (for a meat version)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Pineapple

Cut the pineapple: If using a whole pineapple, slice it in half lengthwise and hollow out one half to use as a bowl. Dice the flesh into small chunks and set aside. You can also use canned pineapple chunks if fresh is unavailable.

Step 2: Prepare the Rice

Use day-old rice: Day-old rice works best because it is drier and will fry better. If you're using freshly cooked rice, let it cool down and refrigerate it for a while to firm it up. Fluff the rice to break up any clumps.

Step 3: Cook the Protein (Optional)

Shrimp or chicken: Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp or diced chicken, stir-fry until cooked through (about 3-4 minutes). Remove and set aside.

Step 4: Scramble the Eggs

Cook the eggs: In the same wok or skillet, add a little more oil if necessary. Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble them until just cooked. Remove and set aside with the shrimp or chicken.

Step 5: Stir-fry the Vegetables

Saute onions and garlic: Add another tablespoon of oil to the wok and stir-fry the chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant, about 2 minutes.

Add vegetables: Stir in the frozen peas and carrots (or mixed vegetables) and cook for another 2 minutes until softened.

Step 6: Stir-fry the Rice

Add the rice: Increase the heat to high and add the cooked, fluffed rice to the wok. Stir-fry the rice for 3-4 minutes, breaking up any clumps and allowing it to absorb the flavors.

Season the rice: Pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce (if using), sugar, and white pepper. Stir well to coat the rice evenly with the seasoning.

Step 7: Add Pineapple and Toppings

Add pineapple chunks: Gently stir in the pineapple chunks, scrambled eggs, and cooked shrimp or chicken. Toss everything together until well combined.

Green onions and nuts: Add chopped green onions and roasted cashews or peanuts for crunch. Toss gently to mix.

Step 8: Serve

Serve in the pineapple bowl: If you hollowed out a pineapple, spoon the fried rice into the pineapple shell for a fun and festive presentation. Garnish with extra green onions or sliced chilies.

Tips for Success:

  • Cold rice works best: Using day-old or refrigerated rice helps prevent it from becoming mushy when stir-frying.
  • Sweet and savory balance: The sweetness of pineapple should complement the savory flavors of soy sauce and oyster sauce. Adjust seasoning as needed to balance the dish.
  • Protein options: This recipe works well with shrimp, chicken, ham, or even tofu for a vegetarian version.

This Chinese-style Pineapple Fried Rice is a flavorful and vibrant dish, combining the freshness of pineapple with savory stir-fried rice, vegetables, and your choice of protein. It's perfect as a main or side dish!
