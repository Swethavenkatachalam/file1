Linguine Aglio e Olio

A traditional Italian-style recipe for Linguine Aglio e Olio (Linguine with Garlic and Olive Oil), a staple of Italian cuisine that highlights simplicity and bold flavors.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) linguine
  • 6-8 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes (optional for a little heat)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • Lemon zest (optional, for extra freshness)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Cook the Linguine
    1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add a generous amount of salt.
    2. Cook the linguine according to the package instructions until al dente (firm to the bite), usually about 8-10 minutes.
    3. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Garlic and Olive Oil Sauce
    1. While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
    2. Add the thinly sliced garlic and saut√© for 2-3 minutes until golden and fragrant, stirring constantly to avoid burning.
    3. If desired, add red pepper flakes for a little heat.
  3. Step 3: Combine Linguine and Sauce
    1. Once the garlic is ready, add the drained linguine directly to the skillet.
    2. Toss everything together, ensuring the pasta is coated with the garlic and oil mixture.
    3. Add a small amount of the reserved pasta water, a little at a time, to create a smooth sauce that lightly coats the pasta.
  4. Step 4: Season and Garnish
    1. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
    2. Stir in the chopped fresh parsley.
    3. Optionally, add a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese or some lemon zest for extra flavor.
  5. Step 5: Serve
    1. Serve immediately, and if desired, finish with an extra drizzle of olive oil or more Parmesan.

Tips

  • Use high-quality extra virgin olive oil for the best flavor.
  • The balance between garlic and oil is key; neither should overpower the dish.
