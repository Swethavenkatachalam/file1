Scallop Risotto

This luxurious Italian dish combines the creamy texture of risotto with the delicate flavor of scallops. Perfect for a special dinner or a sophisticated meal, this recipe highlights the elegance of Italian cuisine.

Ingredients

For the Risotto:

  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice (or Carnaroli rice)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups chicken or seafood stock (hot)
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Scallops:

  • 12 large sea scallops (cleaned and patted dry)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)

For Garnish (Optional):

  • Lemon zest
  • Microgreens or additional chopped parsley
  • Extra Parmesan cheese

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Risotto
    1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large skillet or saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 4-5 minutes.
    2. Toast the Rice: Add 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice to the pan and cook, stirring frequently, until the rice edges become translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
    3. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in 1/2 cup dry white wine and cook, stirring, until the wine is mostly absorbed.
    4. Add Stock Gradually: Begin adding the hot stock, 1/2 cup at a time, stirring constantly and allowing the liquid to be absorbed before adding more. Continue this process until the rice is creamy and cooked to your desired level of doneness, about 18-20 minutes.
    5. Finish the Risotto: Stir in 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese and 2 tablespoons unsalted butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Step 2: Cook the Scallops
    1. Prepare the Scallops: Pat the scallops dry with paper towels to ensure a good sear. Season with salt and pepper.
    2. Sear the Scallops: Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil and 1 tablespoon unsalted butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil and butter are hot and shimmering, add the scallops. Sear the scallops for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they develop a golden-brown crust and are cooked through but still tender.
    3. Add Garlic and Lemon: Add 1 minced garlic clove to the pan during the last minute of cooking, stirring to combine. Drizzle 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice over the scallops, then remove them from the pan and set aside.
  3. Step 3: Serve
    1. Plate the Risotto: Spoon the creamy risotto onto plates or into bowls.
    2. Top with Scallops: Arrange the seared scallops on top of the risotto.
    3. Garnish: Garnish with chopped fresh parsley, lemon zest, and additional Parmesan cheese if desired.
    4. Serve Immediately: Serve the scallop risotto while hot and enjoy!

Tips for Success

  • Use Fresh Scallops: For the best flavor and texture, use fresh, high-quality sea scallops. Avoid pre-frozen scallops if possible, as they can be less tender.
  • Properly Dry Scallops: Patting the scallops dry before searing is crucial for achieving a nice, caramelized crust.
  • Consistent Stirring: Stirring the risotto constantly helps release the rice’s starch, creating a creamy texture.
  • Maintain Warm Stock: Keep the stock warm on the stove while making the risotto. Adding cold stock can slow down the cooking process.
  • Adjust Consistency: If the risotto becomes too thick, you can add a bit more stock or warm water to reach your desired consistency.
