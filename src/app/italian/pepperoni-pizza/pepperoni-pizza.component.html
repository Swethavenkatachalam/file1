Spicy Salami Pizza (Pizza con Salame Piccante)

This authentic Italian-style spicy salami pizza is the closest equivalent to what’s called pepperoni pizza in the U.S. It features a flavorful blend of tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, and spicy salami.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 500g (3.5 cups) Italian 00 flour (or all-purpose flour)
  • 325ml (1 1/3 cups) warm water
  • 10g (2 tsp) salt
  • 3g (1 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil

For the Toppings:

  • 400g (14 oz) canned San Marzano tomatoes (or good-quality whole peeled tomatoes)
  • 250g (9 oz) fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced or torn
  • 100g (3.5 oz) spicy salami (salame piccante), thinly sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)
  • Extra virgin olive oil (for drizzling)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Red pepper flakes (optional for extra heat)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Dough
    1. In a small bowl, mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
    2. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Slowly add the yeast mixture to the flour while mixing with a wooden spoon or hands. Add the olive oil and continue mixing until the dough comes together.
    3. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
    4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 1.5-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Tomato Sauce
    1. Crush or blend the canned San Marzano tomatoes. Add a pinch of salt and a drizzle of olive oil for extra flavor.
    2. Optional: For a thicker sauce, cook the crushed tomatoes in a saucepan over low heat for about 10-15 minutes to reduce some of the liquid.
  3. Step 3: Shape the Pizza
    1. Preheat your oven to its highest setting (usually around 250°C/480°F) for at least 30 minutes. If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven while preheating.
    2. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two or more portions, depending on the desired size. On a floured surface, stretch each portion by hand to form a thin, round pizza base, about 10-12 inches in diameter.
  4. Step 4: Assemble the Pizza
    1. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the pizza base, leaving a small border for the crust.
    2. Distribute the slices or torn pieces of fresh mozzarella evenly over the sauce.
    3. Place the slices of spicy salami (salame piccante) over the cheese.
    4. Drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil over the top, and season with a pinch of salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if desired).
  5. Step 5: Bake the Pizza
    1. Transfer the pizza onto a preheated pizza stone or baking sheet. Bake in the hot oven for 8-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden, the cheese is bubbly, and the salami is slightly crisp around the edges.
    2. If you want extra crispiness, you can broil the pizza for the last 1-2 minutes of baking.
  6. Step 6: Serve
    1. Remove the pizza from the oven and garnish with fresh basil leaves (optional).
    2. Slice the pizza and enjoy immediately.

Tips for Success

  • Use quality salami: Italian spicy salami or salsiccia piccante is key to getting that authentic flavor. Look for thinly sliced, high-quality varieties.
  • Pizza stone: If you have a pizza stone, it helps mimic a traditional wood-fired oven, giving the pizza a crispier crust.
  • High oven temperature: Make sure your oven is as hot as possible to replicate the traditional Neapolitan-style pizza.
