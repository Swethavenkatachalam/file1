Fruity Biscotti with Blueberries

Biscotti are twice-baked Italian cookies known for their crunchy texture. Adding dried blueberries gives a fruity twist to the traditional recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp almond extract (optional)
  • 1/2 cup dried blueberries
  • 1/2 cup slivered almonds (optional)
  • Zest of 1 lemon (optional, for a citrus touch)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Dough
    1. Preheat oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.
    2. Mix dry ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
    3. Cream butter and sugar: In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar together until light and fluffy, about 2-3 minutes.
    4. Add eggs and flavorings: Beat in the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract and almond extract (if using).
    5. Combine dry ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until just combined.
    6. Fold in blueberries: Gently fold in the dried blueberries and slivered almonds (if using). If adding lemon zest, fold it in as well.
  2. Step 2: Shape the Biscotti
    1. Shape the dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and divide it in half. Shape each half into a log about 10-12 inches long and 2-3 inches wide. Transfer the logs onto the prepared baking sheet.
    2. Flatten slightly: Gently press down on the tops of the logs to slightly flatten them.
  3. Step 3: First Bake
    1. Bake the logs: Bake the logs in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the logs are lightly golden and firm to the touch.
    2. Cool slightly: Remove the baking sheet from the oven and allow the logs to cool for about 10 minutes. Reduce the oven temperature to 300°F (150°C).
  4. Step 4: Slice and Second Bake
    1. Slice the logs: Once the logs have cooled slightly, use a sharp serrated knife to slice them diagonally into 1/2-inch thick slices.
    2. Second bake: Place the biscotti slices cut-side down on the baking sheet and return them to the oven. Bake for an additional 10-15 minutes on each side, or until the biscotti are dry and crispy.
  5. Step 5: Cool and Serve
    1. Cool completely: Allow the biscotti to cool completely on a wire rack. They will continue to harden as they cool.
    2. Serve: Serve the biscotti with coffee, tea, or as a snack on their own.

Tips for Success

  • Twice Baking: The second bake is essential to give biscotti their characteristic crunchy texture. Adjust the time based on how crispy you like them.
  • Dried Fruit: If you can’t find dried blueberries, dried cranberries or cherries are excellent substitutes.
  • Storage: Store biscotti in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two weeks. They also freeze well.
