Tiramisu

Tiramisu is one of the most beloved Italian desserts, known for its layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, creamy mascarpone filling, and dusting of cocoa. Here's a classic recipe for making Tiramisu in Italian style.

Ingredients

For the Coffee Mixture:

  • 1 1/2 cups strong espresso coffee, cooled
  • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (such as Kahl√∫a, optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar

For the Mascarpone Cream:

  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cups mascarpone cheese (cold)
  • 1 cup heavy cream (cold)

For Assembly:

  • 24-30 Savoiardi (ladyfingers)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Coffee Mixture

  1. Brew 1 1/2 cups of strong espresso and let it cool to room temperature.
  2. Stir in 2 tablespoons of coffee liqueur (optional) and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Set aside to cool completely.

Step 2: Make the Mascarpone Cream

  1. In a heatproof bowl, combine 4 egg yolks and 1/2 cup sugar. Place the bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler) and whisk constantly for about 5-7 minutes, or until the mixture thickens and becomes pale yellow.
  2. Remove the egg mixture from heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Stir in 1 teaspoon vanilla extract.
  3. In a separate bowl, whip 1 cup heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Set aside.
  4. In another bowl, gently whisk 1 1/4 cups mascarpone cheese until smooth. Fold the cooled egg mixture into the mascarpone until combined.
  5. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture, ensuring the mixture stays light and airy.

Step 3: Assemble the Tiramisu

  1. Quickly dip the Savoiardi (ladyfingers) into the cooled coffee mixture, one at a time. Do not soak them. Place a layer of dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a 9x9 inch (or similar) dish.
  2. Spread half of the mascarpone cream mixture evenly over the layer of ladyfingers.
  3. Add another layer of coffee-dipped ladyfingers on top of the mascarpone cream. Then, spread the remaining mascarpone cream on top.

Step 4: Chill and Serve

  1. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight.
  2. Before serving, dust the top generously with unsweetened cocoa powder. Optionally, add dark chocolate shavings for extra richness.
  3. Slice and serve the tiramisu chilled.

Tips for Success

  • Use Fresh Espresso: Freshly brewed espresso provides the best flavor for a classic tiramisu.
  • Do Not Over-soak Ladyfingers: Dip them quickly in the coffee mixture to avoid sogginess.
  • Chill Well: Allow the tiramisu to chill for several hours or overnight to let the flavors meld and the texture set.
  • Egg Safety: Ensure the egg yolks are cooked safely, or use pasteurized eggs if preferred.
  • Optional Add-ins: Add a layer of cocoa powder or grated chocolate in the middle for extra indulgence.
