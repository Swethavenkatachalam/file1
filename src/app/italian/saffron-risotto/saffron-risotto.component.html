Saffron Risotto with Lobster

This luxurious Italian dish combines creamy risotto with the delicate flavor of lobster and the rich, golden hue of saffron. Perfect for special occasions or a sophisticated dinner, this recipe is both elegant and flavorful.

Ingredients

For the Lobster:

  • 2 whole lobsters (about 1.5 to 2 pounds each)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine (dry)
  • 1 cup lobster stock (or seafood stock)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)

For the Risotto:

  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice (or Carnaroli rice)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups chicken or seafood stock (hot)
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Lobster
    1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the lobsters and cook for about 8-10 minutes, or until the shells turn bright red. Remove the lobsters and transfer them to an ice bath to stop the cooking. Let them cool.
    2. Once cool, remove the lobster meat from the shells. You can save the shells for making lobster stock if desired. Cut the lobster meat into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Lobster Stock
    1. Make lobster stock (optional): If you have lobster shells, simmer them with 4 cups water and 1/2 cup white wine for about 30 minutes to make a flavorful stock. Strain and use this stock in place of or in addition to seafood stock.
  3. Step 3: Make the Risotto
    1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large skillet or saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 4-5 minutes.
    2. Toast the Rice: Add 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice to the pan and cook, stirring frequently, until the edges of the rice become translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
    3. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in 1/2 cup dry white wine and cook, stirring, until the wine is mostly absorbed.
    4. Add Saffron: In a small bowl, dissolve 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads in 1/4 cup hot stock and stir into the rice mixture.
    5. Add Stock Gradually: Begin adding the hot stock, 1/2 cup at a time, stirring constantly and allowing the liquid to be absorbed before adding more. Continue this process until the rice is creamy and cooked to your desired level of doneness, about 18-20 minutes.
    6. Finish the Risotto: Stir in 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese and 2 tablespoons unsalted butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Step 4: Prepare the Lobster
    1. Sauté the Lobster Meat: In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon olive oil and 1 tablespoon unsalted butter over medium heat. Add 2 cloves minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant. Add the lobster meat and cook for 2-3 minutes, then deglaze with 1/2 cup white wine. Allow it to reduce slightly.
    2. Combine with Risotto: Gently fold the cooked lobster meat into the finished risotto.
  5. Step 5: Serve
    1. Plate the Risotto: Spoon the saffron risotto with lobster onto plates or into bowls.
    2. Garnish: Garnish with chopped fresh parsley and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil if desired. Optionally, add a few extra shavings of Parmesan cheese on top.

Tips for Success

  • Use Fresh Lobster: Fresh lobster meat will give the dish the best flavor. If using frozen lobster, ensure it is properly thawed and well-drained.
  • Stir Constantly: Stirring the risotto frequently helps release the rice’s starch, creating a creamy texture.
  • Maintain Warm Stock: Keep the stock warm on the stove while making the risotto. Adding cold stock can slow down the cooking process.
  • Adjust Consistency: If the risotto becomes too thick, you can add a bit more stock or warm water to reach your desired consistency.
  • Avoid Overcooking: Be careful not to overcook the risotto or lobster. The risotto should be creamy and the lobster should be tender but not rubbery.
