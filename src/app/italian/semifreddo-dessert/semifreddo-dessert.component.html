Blackberry Semifreddo

This refreshing Italian dessert is creamy, light, and full of fruity flavors. Semifreddo, which translates to "half-frozen," has the texture of frozen mousse and is perfect for warmer days.

Ingredients

For the Blackberry Purée:

  • 2 cups fresh blackberries (or frozen, thawed)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the Semifreddo:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream (chilled)
  • 1/2 cup mascarpone cheese (optional, for extra creaminess)

For Garnish:

  • Fresh blackberries (for serving)
  • Mint leaves (optional)
  • Powdered sugar (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Blackberry Purée
    1. In a small saucepan, combine 2 cups blackberries, 1/4 cup granulated sugar, and 1 tablespoon lemon juice. Cook over medium heat for about 5-7 minutes until the blackberries break down and the mixture thickens slightly.
    2. Transfer the cooked blackberries to a blender or food processor and blend until smooth. Strain the purée through a fine-mesh sieve to remove seeds and set aside to cool completely.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Egg Mixture
    1. Set up a double boiler: Bring a small pot of water to a simmer. In a heatproof bowl, combine 3 large eggs, 3 large egg yolks, and 1 cup granulated sugar.
    2. Place the bowl over the simmering water and whisk constantly until the mixture thickens and becomes pale, about 5-7 minutes (it should reach 160°F/70°C). Remove from heat and continue whisking for 2-3 minutes to cool slightly. Stir in 1 teaspoon vanilla extract and set aside to cool completely.
  3. Step 3: Whip the Cream
    1. In a large bowl, whip 1 1/2 cups chilled heavy cream with an electric mixer until soft peaks form. If using mascarpone cheese, fold in 1/2 cup for extra richness.
    2. Gently fold the cooled egg mixture into the whipped cream until well combined.
  4. Step 4: Assemble the Semifreddo
    1. Gently fold 2/3 of the blackberry purée into the semifreddo mixture to create a marbled effect. Reserve the remaining purée for serving.
    2. Line a loaf pan with plastic wrap, leaving some overhang. Pour the semifreddo mixture into the pan, smooth the top, and cover with plastic wrap. Freeze for at least 6 hours or overnight.
  5. Step 5: Serve
    1. When ready to serve, remove the semifreddo from the freezer and unmold it by lifting it out of the pan using the plastic wrap. Place it on a serving platter.
    2. Drizzle the remaining blackberry purée over the top, garnish with fresh blackberries, mint leaves, and a dusting of powdered sugar if desired. Slice and serve immediately.

Tips for Success

  • Egg Safety: Ensure the egg mixture is cooked to 160°F (70°C) to safely eliminate any risk from raw eggs.
  • Whipping the Cream: Avoid overwhipping the cream. You want soft peaks for a light, airy texture.
  • Folding Technique: Use a gentle folding motion when incorporating the egg mixture and blackberry purée to preserve the airiness of the semifreddo.
  • Serving: Allow the semifreddo to sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes before slicing for the best texture.
