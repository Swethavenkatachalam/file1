Pasta ai Quattro Formaggi

This rich and indulgent Italian-style Four Cheese Pasta features a blend of four cheeses melted into a creamy sauce. It’s a decadent dish that highlights the beautiful harmony of Italy’s finest cheeses.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) pasta (penne, rigatoni, or fettuccine work well)
  • 100g (3.5 oz) Gorgonzola cheese, crumbled
  • 100g (3.5 oz) Fontina cheese, diced or grated
  • 100g (3.5 oz) Parmigiano-Reggiano (Parmesan), freshly grated
  • 100g (3.5 oz) Pecorino Romano, freshly grated
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Cook the Pasta
    1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and season it generously with salt.
    2. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
    3. Before draining, reserve about 1/2 cup of the pasta cooking water.
    4. Drain the pasta and set aside.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Quattro Formaggi Sauce
    1. In a large skillet or saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
    2. Once the butter is melted, add the heavy cream and let it heat gently for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently.
    3. Gradually add the Gorgonzola, Fontina, Parmigiano-Reggiano, and Pecorino Romano cheeses. Stir continuously until the cheeses melt into the cream and form a smooth, creamy sauce.
  3. Step 3: Combine the Pasta and Sauce
    1. Add the drained pasta directly into the skillet with the cheese sauce. Toss everything together to coat the pasta evenly.
    2. If the sauce is too thick, add a little reserved pasta water, a spoonful at a time, until you reach the desired consistency.
    3. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt (if needed) and freshly ground black pepper.
  4. Step 4: Serve
    1. Transfer the pasta to serving plates or a large dish.
    2. Optionally, sprinkle with fresh parsley or an extra grating of Parmigiano-Reggiano for added flavor and presentation.

Tips for Success

  • Balance the cheeses: Gorgonzola adds a tangy and strong flavor, while Fontina is mild and creamy. Parmigiano and Pecorino bring a sharp, nutty quality. Adjust the quantities depending on your taste.
  • Pasta choices: Use short pasta shapes like penne or rigatoni that hold the rich sauce well, or opt for fettuccine for a classic pairing.
  • Creaminess: For a lighter sauce, reduce the amount of heavy cream or use a mixture of milk and cream.
