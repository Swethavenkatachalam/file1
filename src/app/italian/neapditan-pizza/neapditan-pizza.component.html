Neapolitan Meat Sauce (Ragù alla Napoletana)

This classic Italian-style Neapolitan Meat Sauce is rich, hearty, and traditionally served over pasta, like spaghetti or ziti. It’s made with large cuts of meat rather than ground beef.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) beef chuck, pork shoulder, or veal (or a mix), cut into large chunks
  • 100g (3.5 oz) pancetta, diced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 cans (800g or 28 oz each) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup dry red wine
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Fresh basil leaves, a small handful
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Brown the Meat
    1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
    2. Add the chunks of meat and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes per side. Remove the meat from the pot and set aside.
  2. Step 2: Sauté the Pancetta, Onion, and Garlic
    1. Add the diced pancetta to the same pot and cook for 3-4 minutes until crispy.
    2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until soft and golden, about 5-6 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Step 3: Deglaze the Pot
    1. Pour in the red wine, scraping the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits. Let the wine simmer for 2-3 minutes until reduced slightly.
  4. Step 4: Simmer the Sauce
    1. Stir in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste. Mix well to combine.
    2. Add the browned meat back into the pot, ensuring it's submerged in the sauce.
    3. Add salt, pepper, the bay leaf, and fresh basil leaves. If the tomatoes are too acidic, add a pinch of sugar to balance it out.
  5. Step 5: Slow-Cook the Ragù
    1. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the sauce simmer gently for about 2-3 hours. Stir occasionally, and check to ensure the sauce doesn’t stick to the bottom.
    2. The meat should become tender and easily pull apart with a fork.
  6. Step 6: Serve
    1. Remove the meat from the sauce. Serve it whole or shred it into the sauce.
    2. Serve the sauce over pasta and garnish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and additional fresh basil if desired.

Tips for Success

  • Use high-quality meat: A mix of beef, pork, or veal adds depth of flavor.
  • Slow cooking: The longer the sauce simmers, the richer the flavors become. Be patient!
  • Pairing: This ragù goes beautifully with large pasta shapes like ziti, paccheri, or even gnocchi.

Pasta ai Quattro Formaggi

Pasta ai Quattro Formaggi (Four Cheese Pasta) is a rich and indulgent Italian dish featuring a blend of four cheeses melted into a creamy sauce.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) pasta (penne, rigatoni, or fettuccine work well)
  • 100g (3.5 oz) Gorgonzola cheese, crumbled
  • 100g (3.5 oz) Fontina cheese, diced or grated
  • 100g (3.5 oz) Parmigiano-Reggiano (Parmesan), freshly grated
  • 100g (3.5 oz) Pecorino Romano, freshly grated
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Cook the Pasta
    1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and season it generously with salt.
    2. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve about 1/2 cup of the pasta cooking water before draining.
    3. Drain the pasta and set aside.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Quattro Formaggi Sauce
    1. In a large skillet or saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
    2. Add the heavy cream and heat gently for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently.
    3. Gradually add the Gorgonzola, Fontina, Parmigiano-Reggiano, and Pecorino Romano cheeses. Stir continuously until the cheeses melt into the cream and form a smooth, creamy sauce.
  3. Step 3: Combine the Pasta and Sauce
    1. Add the drained pasta directly into the skillet with the cheese sauce. Toss to coat the pasta evenly.
    2. If the sauce is too thick, add a little reserved pasta water, a spoonful at a time, until you reach the desired consistency.
    3. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  4. Step 4: Serve
    1. Transfer the pasta to serving plates or a large dish.
    2. Optionally, garnish with fresh parsley or an extra grating of Parmigiano-Reggiano.

Tips for Success

  • Balance the cheeses: Gorgonzola adds a tangy flavor, while Fontina is mild and creamy. Parmigiano and Pecorino bring a sharp, nutty quality. Adjust quantities to taste.
  • Pasta choices: Use short pasta shapes like penne or rigatoni that hold the rich sauce well, or opt for fettuccine for a classic pairing.
  • Creaminess: For a lighter sauce, reduce the amount of heavy cream or use a mixture of milk and cream.
