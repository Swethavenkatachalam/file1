Vermicelli alla Mediterranea

Hereâ€™s an Italian-style recipe for Vermicelli pasta, prepared simply with fresh ingredients, highlighting the lightness and texture of the vermicelli noodles. This dish is known for its balance between the mild flavors of garlic, olive oil, and tomatoes, with a touch of herbs.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) vermicelli pasta
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 250g (9 oz) cherry tomatoes, halved (or peeled and chopped Roma tomatoes)
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional for a bit of heat)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese (optional)
  • Lemon zest (optional, for freshness)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Vermicelli

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the vermicelli and cook according to the package instructions until al dente, usually 5-7 minutes.
  3. Reserve about 1/2 cup of the pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.

Step 2: Prepare the Tomato and Garlic Sauce

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the thinly sliced garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant and golden. Be careful not to let it burn.
  2. Add the halved cherry tomatoes (or chopped Roma tomatoes) to the skillet. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the tomatoes soften and release their juices.
  3. Add a pinch of salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir occasionally and let the sauce simmer for 5-6 minutes.

Step 3: Combine Pasta and Sauce

  1. Add the cooked vermicelli directly into the skillet with the tomato and garlic sauce.
  2. Gently toss the pasta in the sauce, ensuring it's well coated. If the sauce seems too thick, add a little of the reserved pasta water to thin it out and help the sauce cling to the pasta.

Step 4: Season and Garnish

  1. Stir in the chopped basil and parsley for a fresh, aromatic flavor.
  2. Taste and adjust with more salt and pepper if needed.
  3. Optionally, sprinkle grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese over the top and add a touch of lemon zest for brightness.

Step 5: Serve

  1. Transfer the vermicelli to plates or a large serving dish.
  2. Drizzle a bit more extra virgin olive oil over the pasta just before serving for extra richness.

Tips for Success

  • Use fresh ingredients: The simplicity of this dish relies on the freshness of the tomatoes, garlic, and herbs.
  • Al dente pasta: Vermicelli is a thin pasta, so keep an eye on it while cooking to prevent it from becoming too soft.
  • Add proteins: You can also add shrimp, chicken, or anchovies to make the dish heartier.
