Mini Panettone

Mini Panettone is a smaller version of the classic Italian Christmas bread, traditionally filled with dried fruits and citrus zest. Making mini panettone at home is a great way to share this festive treat with individual servings.

Ingredients (Makes 6-8 Mini Panettones)

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1/2 cup warm milk (110°F/45°C)
  • 2 large eggs (room temperature)
  • 2 large egg yolks (room temperature)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon orange zest
  • 1/2 cup raisins or currants (soaked in warm water or rum for 30 minutes)
  • 1/2 cup candied orange peel (optional)
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon honey

For Brushing:

  • 1 egg yolk (beaten with 1 tablespoon milk)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Yeast
    1. In a small bowl, dissolve 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast in 1/2 cup warm milk (110°F/45°C). Stir in 1 tablespoon of honey and let it sit for 5-10 minutes, or until the mixture becomes frothy.
  2. Step 2: Make the Dough
    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 3 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/3 cup granulated sugar, and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Set aside.
    2. In another bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 2 large egg yolks, 1 teaspoon vanilla extract, 1 teaspoon lemon zest, and 1 teaspoon orange zest.
    3. Add the activated yeast mixture and the egg mixture into the bowl of dry ingredients. Stir until it forms a sticky dough.
    4. Gradually add in 1/2 cup softened unsalted butter, one tablespoon at a time, mixing continuously. Knead by hand or use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment until the dough is smooth, elastic, and slightly sticky, about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Step 3: Add the Fruits
    1. Drain the soaked raisins or currants, and pat them dry with a paper towel. Toss with a bit of flour to prevent sticking.
    2. Gently fold in the raisins, candied orange peel, and dried cranberries (if using) into the dough until evenly distributed.
  4. Step 4: First Rise
    1. Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap or a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  5. Step 5: Shape the Mini Panettones
    1. Use mini panettone paper molds or muffin tins lined with parchment paper. Lightly grease them.
    2. Punch down the dough and divide it into equal portions (about 6-8 pieces, depending on the size of your molds). Shape each portion into a smooth ball and place them into the prepared molds, filling them about halfway.
  6. Step 6: Second Rise
    1. Cover the mini panettones with a clean kitchen towel and allow them to rise again in a warm spot for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the dough has puffed up and nearly reaches the top of the molds.
  7. Step 7: Bake the Mini Panettones
    1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
    2. Lightly brush the tops of the mini panettones with the egg yolk and milk mixture for a shiny, golden finish.
    3. Bake for about 20-25 minutes, or until the tops are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. If they brown too quickly, you can tent them with aluminum foil halfway through the baking time.
  8. Step 8: Cool and Serve
    1. Once baked, remove the mini panettones from the oven and allow them to cool on a wire rack.
    2. Serve your mini panettones slightly warm or at room temperature. They can be enjoyed plain, with butter, or alongside a cup of coffee or tea.

Tips for Success

  • Dough Texture: Panettone dough is rich and slightly sticky due to the butter and eggs. Make sure to knead it well until smooth and elastic.
  • Add-ins: Feel free to customize the fruit mix. You can use chocolate chips, nuts, or other dried fruits if you prefer.
  • Storage: Store the mini panettones in an airtight container for up to 4 days. They also freeze well; wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and store in a freezer-safe bag for up to 1 month.
