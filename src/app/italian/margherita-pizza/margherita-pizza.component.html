Pizza Margherita

An authentic Pizza Margherita recipe, the classic Italian pizza topped with tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, basil, and olive oil. This pizza embodies the colors of the Italian flag and is loved for its simplicity and balance.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 500g (3.5 cups) Italian 00 flour (or all-purpose flour)
  • 325ml (1 1/3 cup) warm water
  • 10g (2 tsp) salt
  • 3g (1 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil

For the Toppings:

  • 400g (14 oz) canned San Marzano tomatoes (or good-quality whole peeled tomatoes)
  • 250g (9 oz) fresh mozzarella cheese (preferably mozzarella di bufala)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil (for drizzling)
  • Salt (to taste)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Dough
    1. In a small bowl, mix the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
    2. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the yeast mixture, mixing with a wooden spoon or your hands.
    3. Add the olive oil and continue mixing until the dough starts to come together.
    4. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
    5. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1.5-2 hours until it doubles in size.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Tomato Sauce
    1. Pour the canned San Marzano tomatoes into a bowl and crush by hand or blend for a smooth sauce.
    2. Optional: Cook the crushed tomatoes in a saucepan over low heat for 10-15 minutes to reduce excess liquid.
  3. Step 3: Shape the Pizza
    1. Preheat your oven to the highest setting (250°C/480°F or higher) for at least 30 minutes. Place a pizza stone in the oven if you have one.
    2. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two or more portions.
    3. On a floured surface, stretch each dough ball by hand or roll into a thin, round pizza base (10-12 inches).
  4. Step 4: Assemble the Pizza
    1. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the pizza base, leaving a small border for the crust.
    2. Tear fresh mozzarella into pieces and scatter evenly over the sauce.
    3. Add fresh basil leaves and drizzle with extra virgin olive oil.
  5. Step 5: Bake the Pizza
    1. Bake in the oven (or on a pizza stone) for 8-10 minutes, until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
    2. Optional: Broil the pizza for 1-2 minutes for an extra crispy crust.
  6. Step 6: Serve
    1. Remove the pizza from the oven, garnish with fresh basil leaves, and let it cool slightly before slicing.

Tips for Success

  • Use high-quality ingredients like San Marzano tomatoes and buffalo mozzarella for the best flavor.
  • If you have a pizza stone, it helps mimic the heat of a wood-fired oven for a crispier crust.
  • Preheat your oven to the highest possible temperature for an authentic Neapolitan-style pizza.
