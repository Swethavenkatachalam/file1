Bean and Cheese Burrito Recipe

A Bean and Cheese Burrito is a classic Mexican dish that's simple, delicious, and comforting. It features refried beans and melted cheese wrapped in a warm tortilla. Here’s a traditional recipe for a Mexican Bean and Cheese Burrito.

Ingredients:

For the Burrito Filling:

  • 1 can (15 oz) refried beans (or 2 cups homemade refried beans)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a Mexican blend)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (for cooking the beans, if needed)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Assembly:

  • 4 large flour tortillas (10-12 inch)
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro (optional)
  • 1/2 cup salsa or pico de gallo (optional, for serving)
  • 1/4 cup sour cream (optional, for serving)
  • 1/4 cup guacamole (optional, for serving)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Beans

  1. Heat the beans: If using canned refried beans, heat them in a small pot over medium heat. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. If the beans are too thick, add a bit of water to reach your desired consistency.
  2. Season the beans: Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. If using homemade refried beans, follow the same steps to warm and season them.

Step 2: Warm the Tortillas

  1. Heat the tortillas: Warm the flour tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat for about 30 seconds on each side, or microwave them for a few seconds until they are pliable.

Step 3: Assemble the Burritos

  1. Spread the beans: Place a warmed tortilla on a flat surface. Spread a generous portion of the warm refried beans down the center of the tortilla.
  2. Add cheese: Sprinkle shredded cheese over the beans. The heat from the beans will begin to melt the cheese slightly.
  3. Optional toppings: Add chopped cilantro, salsa, or other desired toppings before rolling, if using.

Step 4: Roll the Burritos

  1. Fold and roll: Fold the sides of the tortilla over the filling, then fold the bottom up and roll tightly from the bottom to the top to enclose the filling.

Step 5: Optional – Crisp the Burritos

  1. Grill or pan-fry: For a crispy texture, heat a skillet over medium heat and place the rolled burritos seam-side down. Cook for 1-2 minutes on each side until the tortilla is golden brown and crisp.

Step 6: Serve

Serve hot: Serve the burritos with additional salsa, sour cream, and guacamole on the side if desired.

Tips for Success:

  • Customize your beans: You can use black beans, pinto beans, or any type of beans you prefer. If using whole beans, mash them slightly before using.
  • Cheese: Choose a cheese that melts well for the best texture. Mexican blend cheese or Monterey Jack are great options.
  • Tortilla size: Ensure you use large tortillas to hold all the filling without tearing.

This Bean and Cheese Burrito is a satisfying and easy-to-make meal that captures the essence of traditional Mexican flavors in a simple and delicious form. Enjoy!
