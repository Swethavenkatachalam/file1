Nopales and Tomatillo Stew (Sopa de Nopales con Tomatillo)

Nopales and Tomatillo Stew is a flavorful and nutritious Mexican dish featuring nopales (cactus pads) and tomatillos in a savory broth. It’s a great option for those looking to explore traditional Mexican cuisine with a healthy twist.

Ingredients

For the Stew:

  • 2 cups nopales (cactus pads), cleaned and sliced into strips
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4-5 tomatillos, husked and chopped (or 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatillos)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (or chicken broth)
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Garnishing:

  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled queso fresco or shredded cheese
  • Lime wedges
  • Sliced radishes (optional)
  • Sour cream (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Nopales

  1. Clean the nopales: Rinse the nopales under cold water. Using a knife, remove any thorns and tough edges. Slice the nopales into thin strips.
  2. Cook the nopales: In a pot of boiling water, cook the nopales for 10 minutes until they become tender and the color changes to a darker green. Drain and rinse under cold water to remove excess sliminess. Set aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Stew Base

  1. Sauté aromatics: In a large pot, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute.
  2. Add tomatillos: Add the chopped tomatillos to the pot and cook for 5 minutes, allowing them to soften and release their juices. If using canned tomatillos, add them along with their liquid.

Step 3: Build the Stew

  1. Add broth and tomatoes: Stir in the vegetable broth and diced tomatoes. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  2. Add spices: Stir in the ground cumin, dried oregano, smoked paprika, chili powder (if using), salt, and pepper.
  3. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

Step 4: Add Nopales

  1. Combine and cook: Add the cooked nopales to the pot. Stir to combine and continue to simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the nopales are tender and have absorbed the flavors of the stew.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

  1. Serve: Ladle the stew into bowls. Garnish with fresh cilantro, avocado slices, crumbled queso fresco, and lime wedges. Add sliced radishes and a dollop of sour cream if desired.

Tips for Success

  • Nopales: When cooking nopales, removing excess sliminess by rinsing them under cold water after boiling is essential for the best texture.
  • Tomatillos: Fresh tomatillos add a tangy flavor, but canned tomatillos are a convenient substitute.
  • Adjusting flavor: Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. You can add more chili powder for extra heat or more lime juice for brightness.
