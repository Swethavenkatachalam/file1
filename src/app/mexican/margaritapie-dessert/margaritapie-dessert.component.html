Margarita Pie

Margarita Pie is a creamy and refreshing dessert inspired by the classic margarita cocktail. It combines tangy lime and a hint of tequila in a smooth filling, perfect for a summer treat. Here’s a recipe for making Margarita Pie in a Mexican style.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Filling:

  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup fresh lime juice (about 4-5 limes)
  • 1/4 cup tequila
  • 1 tbsp orange liqueur (such as Triple Sec or Cointreau)
  • 1 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • Zest of 2 limes

For Garnishing (Optional):

  • Whipped cream
  • Lime slices or zest
  • Coarse salt (for rimming, if desired)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. Mix crust ingredients: In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, granulated sugar, and melted butter. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and resemble wet sand.
  2. Press into pan: Press the mixture firmly into the bottom and up the sides of a 9-inch pie dish. Use the back of a spoon or a flat-bottomed glass to ensure it's evenly pressed.
  3. Bake the crust: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the crust for 8-10 minutes, or until lightly golden. Let it cool completely before adding the filling.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

  1. Mix filling ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the sweetened condensed milk, fresh lime juice, tequila, orange liqueur, and sour cream or Greek yogurt until smooth.
  2. Add lime zest: Stir in the lime zest for an extra burst of lime flavor.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

  1. Pour filling into crust: Pour the lime mixture into the cooled graham cracker crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.
  2. Bake the pie: Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the filling is set and just slightly jiggles in the center. The edges should be slightly puffed.

Step 4: Cool and Chill

  1. Cool: Allow the pie to cool at room temperature for about 30 minutes.
  2. Chill: Refrigerate the pie for at least 3 hours, or until completely chilled and set.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

  1. Garnish: If desired, garnish with whipped cream, lime slices or zest, and a sprinkle of coarse salt around the edge of the pie (similar to a margarita glass rim).
  2. Serve: Slice and serve chilled.

Tips for Success

  • Lime Juice: Fresh lime juice is key for the best flavor. Avoid using bottled lime juice if possible.
  • Tequila: Use a good quality tequila for the best taste. If you prefer a non-alcoholic version, you can omit the tequila and add extra lime juice or zest.
  • Chilling Time: Make sure to allow enough time for the pie to chill so that it firms up properly.
