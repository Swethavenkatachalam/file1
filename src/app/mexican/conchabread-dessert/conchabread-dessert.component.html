Conchas (Mexican Sweet Bread)

Conchas are a type of traditional Mexican sweet bread, known for their soft, fluffy interior and unique shell-like topping. They’re perfect for pairing with coffee or hot chocolate. Here’s a recipe to help you make them at home.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 1/4 tsp (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup whole milk, warmed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the Topping:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp cocoa powder (for chocolate topping, optional)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm milk and 1 tbsp of sugar. Sprinkle the yeast over the top and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, remaining sugar, and salt.
  3. Combine wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, beat the eggs, vanilla extract, and softened butter until well combined.
  4. Combine ingredients: Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the yeast mixture and the egg mixture. Mix until a dough forms.
  5. Knead the dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic, or use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment.
  6. First rise: Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap or a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.

Step 2: Prepare the Topping

  1. Mix the topping: In a medium bowl, beat together the softened butter and sugar until creamy. Add the flour, baking powder, and salt, and mix until combined. For chocolate topping, add cocoa powder.
  2. Chill the topping: Divide the topping into 2-3 portions if you’re making different flavors. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate until firm.

Step 3: Shape the Conchas

  1. Shape the dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into 12-16 equal pieces. Shape each piece into a round ball and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  2. Add the topping: Roll out or flatten the chilled topping into circles slightly larger than the dough balls. Place a piece of topping on each dough ball and use a knife or a special concha cutter to create a shell pattern on the topping.

Step 4: Second Rise

  1. Second rise: Cover the dough balls with a damp cloth and let them rise again for 30-45 minutes, or until slightly puffed.

Step 5: Bake the Conchas

  1. Preheat oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Bake: Bake the conchas for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown. The topping should be cracked, and the bread should sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

  1. Cool: Allow the conchas to cool on a wire rack before serving.

Tips for Success

  • Dough Rising: Make sure to give the dough ample time to rise. A warm, draft-free environment helps the dough rise better.
  • Topping Texture: The topping should be firm but pliable. If it’s too soft, it may not hold its shape during baking.
  • Flavor Variations: Experiment with different flavors for the topping by adding spices or extracts to suit your taste.
