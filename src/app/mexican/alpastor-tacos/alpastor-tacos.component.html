Al Pastor Chicken

Al Pastor Chicken is a delicious twist on the traditional Mexican al pastor, which is usually made with pork. This recipe uses chicken marinated in a flavorful blend of spices and pineapple, then grilled to perfection. It’s great for tacos, burritos, or served with rice and beans.

Ingredients:

For the Marinade:

  • 500g (1 lb) chicken thighs (boneless and skinless)
  • 1/4 cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar
  • 2 tbsp adobo sauce (from canned chipotles in adobo)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

For Serving:

  • Warm tortillas (corn or flour)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Diced onions
  • Pineapple slices, grilled or fresh
  • Lime wedges
  • Salsa or pico de gallo

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Marinade

  1. Combine marinade ingredients: In a large bowl, mix together the pineapple juice, white vinegar, adobo sauce, minced garlic, ground cumin, paprika, dried oregano, ground coriander, ground cinnamon, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and black pepper.

Step 2: Marinate the Chicken

  1. Add chicken to marinade: Cut the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces or leave them whole if preferred. Add the chicken to the marinade, ensuring it is well-coated.
  2. Marinate: Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or up to 4 hours for the best flavor.

Step 3: Cook the Chicken

  1. Preheat grill or skillet: Heat a grill or large skillet over medium-high heat. If using a grill, brush the grates with oil to prevent sticking.
  2. Cook the chicken: Remove the chicken from the marinade and discard the excess marinade. Cook the chicken for about 5-7 minutes per side on the grill, or 7-10 minutes in the skillet, until the chicken is cooked through and has a nice char. Internal temperature should reach 75°C (165°F).

Step 4: Prepare the Pineapple

  1. Grill the pineapple: If using fresh pineapple, cut it into rings and grill for about 2-3 minutes per side until caramelized. Alternatively, you can use canned pineapple slices, but grilling adds extra flavor.

Step 5: Assemble and Serve

  1. Slice the chicken: Once cooked, let the chicken rest for a few minutes, then slice or chop it into small pieces.
  2. Warm tortillas: Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave.
  3. Assemble tacos: Place the chicken in the tortillas and top with fresh cilantro, diced onions, and grilled pineapple. Serve with lime wedges and salsa or pico de gallo on the side.

Tips for Success:

  • Marinate time: Marinating the chicken for at least 1 hour enhances the flavor, but longer marination (up to 4 hours) will give even better results.
  • Grilling: For added authenticity, use a grill to get that classic charred flavor. If using a skillet, you can achieve a good sear by cooking over high heat.
  • Pineapple: The pineapple adds sweetness and a tropical flavor that complements the spices. Grilling the pineapple caramelizes its sugars and enhances its flavor.

Al Pastor Chicken is a flavorful, versatile dish that can be enjoyed in tacos, burritos, or on its own with your favorite sides. Enjoy the vibrant flavors of Mexican cuisine with this delicious recipe!
