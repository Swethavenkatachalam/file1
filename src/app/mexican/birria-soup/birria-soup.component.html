Birria

Birria is a traditional Mexican stew, often made with beef, goat, or lamb, that's richly flavored with a blend of spices and chiles. It’s known for its deep, savory flavors and tender meat, commonly served with warm tortillas and various garnishes. Here’s a detailed recipe for making Birria in the traditional Mexican style.

Ingredients:

For the Birria:

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) beef chuck or brisket, cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 dried guajillo chiles
  • 2 dried ancho chiles
  • 1 dried pasilla chile
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar or white vinegar
  • Salt to taste

For Serving:

  • Corn or flour tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Diced onions
  • Lime wedges
  • Salsa or pico de gallo
  • Radishes, sliced (optional)
  • Avocado slices (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Chiles

  1. Toast the chiles: Heat a dry skillet over medium heat. Add the dried guajillo, ancho, and pasilla chiles. Toast them for about 1-2 minutes, turning occasionally, until they become fragrant. Be careful not to burn them.
  2. Soak the chiles: Remove the stems and seeds from the chiles. Place the chiles in a bowl and cover with hot water. Soak for about 15 minutes, or until softened.

Step 2: Make the Chile Paste

  1. Blend the chiles: Drain the chiles and place them in a blender. Add 1 cup of the soaking water, and blend until smooth. You may need to add more water if the paste is too thick. Set aside.

Step 3: Cook the Meat

  1. Sear the meat: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef chunks and sear until browned on all sides. Remove the meat and set aside.
  2. Sauté the aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

Step 4: Combine Ingredients

  1. Add spices: Stir in the ground cumin, dried oregano, smoked paprika, dried thyme, black pepper, ground cinnamon, and ground cloves. Cook for 1-2 minutes to bloom the spices.
  2. Add the chile paste: Stir in the prepared chile paste and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add broth and tomatoes: Pour in the beef broth and tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes. Stir to combine.
  4. Return the meat: Add the seared beef chunks back into the pot. Add the bay leaves and apple cider vinegar. Stir to combine.

Step 5: Simmer

  1. Cook the birria: Bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily shreds with a fork.

Step 6: Shred the Meat

  1. Shred the meat: Once the meat is tender, remove it from the pot and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded meat to the pot and stir to combine.

Step 7: Serve

  1. Prepare tortillas: Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave.
  2. Serve the birria: Ladle the birria into bowls. Serve with warm tortillas on the side. Garnish with fresh cilantro, diced onions, lime wedges, and optional toppings like salsa, radishes, and avocado slices.

Tips for Success:

  • Chiles: Adjust the type and quantity of chiles to suit your taste. You can use more or less depending on your preferred level of heat and smokiness.
  • Meat: Beef chuck or brisket are commonly used, but you can also use goat or lamb for a more traditional flavor.
  • Acidity: The vinegar adds a nice tanginess. You can adjust the amount to taste.

Birria is a rich and flavorful stew that's perfect for any occasion. The combination of tender meat, spicy and savory broth, and fresh garnishes makes it a true Mexican classic. Enjoy!
