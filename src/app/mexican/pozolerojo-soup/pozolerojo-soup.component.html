Pozole Rojo

Pozole Rojo is a traditional Mexican stew made with hominy, pork, and a rich red chili sauce. It’s a hearty and comforting dish, often enjoyed during special occasions and celebrations.

Ingredients

For the Pozole:

  • 2 lbs (900g) pork shoulder or pork ribs, cut into chunks
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) hominy, drained and rinsed (or 1 cup dried hominy, soaked overnight and cooked)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Red Chili Sauce:

  • 4-5 dried guajillo chiles
  • 2-3 dried ancho chiles
  • 1-2 dried pasilla chiles
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup water (for blending)

For Garnishing:

  • Shredded lettuce or cabbage
  • Sliced radishes
  • Diced onions
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Lime wedges
  • Sliced avocado
  • Crumbled queso fresco (optional)
  • Warm tortillas or tortilla chips

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Red Chili Sauce

  1. Toast the chiles: Heat a dry skillet over medium heat. Add the dried guajillo, ancho, and pasilla chiles. Toast them for about 1-2 minutes, turning occasionally, until fragrant. Be careful not to burn them.
  2. Soak the chiles: Remove the stems and seeds from the chiles. Place them in a bowl and cover with hot water. Soak for about 15 minutes until softened.
  3. Blend the chiles: Drain the chiles and place them in a blender with 2 cloves of garlic and 1/2 cup of water. Blend until smooth. Add more water if needed for a smooth consistency.

Step 2: Cook the Pork

  1. Sear the pork: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the pork chunks and sear until browned on all sides. Remove the pork and set aside.
  2. Sauté aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

Step 3: Build the Pozole

  1. Add spices: Stir in the ground cumin, dried oregano, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for 1-2 minutes to bloom the spices.
  2. Add the chili sauce: Stir in the red chili sauce and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add broth and pork: Pour in the chicken broth and return the seared pork chunks to the pot. Add the bay leaves and stir to combine.
  4. Simmer: Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 1.5-2 hours, or until the pork is tender and easily shreds.

Step 4: Add the Hominy

  1. Add hominy: Stir in the drained and rinsed hominy (or cooked hominy if using dried). Continue to simmer for an additional 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

Step 5: Season and Serve

  1. Adjust seasoning: Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  2. Serve: Ladle the pozole into bowls. Garnish with shredded lettuce or cabbage, sliced radishes, diced onions, chopped cilantro, and lime wedges. Add avocado slices and crumbled queso fresco if desired. Serve with warm tortillas or tortilla chips on the side.

Tips for Success

  • Hominy: Canned hominy is a convenient option, but if using dried hominy, make sure to soak and cook it ahead of time.
  • Chili Sauce: Adjust the amount of dried chiles to control the heat and smokiness of the pozole. Use more or less chiles according to your taste.
  • Pork: You can use a combination of pork shoulder and ribs for added flavor and texture.
