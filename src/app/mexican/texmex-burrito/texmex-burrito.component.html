Tex-Mex Burrito Recipe

Tex-Mex Burritos are a fusion of Texan and Mexican flavors, typically filled with seasoned meat, beans, rice, cheese, and other ingredients wrapped in a large tortilla. Here’s a recipe for a Tex-Mex Burrito with a Mexican flair.

Ingredients:

For the Burrito Filling:

  • 500g (1 lb) ground beef or chicken
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp oregano
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce or salsa
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans or pinto beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels (optional)

For Assembly:

  • 4 large flour tortillas (10-12 inch)
  • 1 cup cooked rice (preferably Mexican-style or cilantro-lime rice)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a Mexican blend)
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup guacamole
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro (optional)
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes or pico de gallo
  • 1 jalapeño, sliced (optional)
  • Hot sauce (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

  1. Sauté the onion and garlic: In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook for 2-3 minutes until softened and fragrant.
  2. Brown the meat: Add the ground beef or chicken to the skillet. Cook until browned and fully cooked, about 5-6 minutes. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  3. Season the meat: Add cumin, chili powder, paprika, oregano, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Add tomato sauce: Stir in the tomato sauce or salsa and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes to thicken slightly.
  5. Mix in the beans and corn: Add the drained black beans or pinto beans and corn (if using) to the skillet. Stir everything together and let it cook for another 2-3 minutes until heated through. Adjust the seasoning as needed.

Step 2: Warm the Tortillas

Heat tortillas: Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave for a few seconds to make them pliable and easier to fold.

Step 3: Assemble the Burritos

  1. Layer the ingredients: Place a warmed tortilla on a flat surface. Start by spreading a spoonful of sour cream and guacamole in the center. Then add a scoop of the cooked rice, followed by a generous portion of the meat and bean filling.
  2. Add cheese and toppings: Sprinkle shredded cheese over the filling, and add diced tomatoes or pico de gallo, sliced jalapeños, and a little chopped cilantro if desired.
  3. Fold the burrito: Fold the sides of the tortilla over the filling, then fold the bottom up and tightly roll the burrito from the bottom to the top, enclosing the filling completely.

Step 4: Heat the Burritos (Optional)

Grill or pan-fry: For a crispy burrito, heat a skillet over medium heat and place the rolled burritos seam-side down. Grill for 1-2 minutes on each side until golden brown and crisp.

Step 5: Serve

Serve hot: Serve the burritos hot, with extra salsa, sour cream, guacamole, or hot sauce on the side.

Tips for Success:

  • Customizable: You can easily customize the fillings by adding sautéed bell peppers, onions, or other vegetables, or swapping the ground meat for shredded chicken or beef.
  • Make it vegetarian: Skip the meat and add more beans, rice, and veggies to make a vegetarian burrito.
  • Keep it warm: If making multiple burritos, keep them warm in the oven at a low temperature while you prepare the rest.

This Tex-Mex Burrito combines savory, spicy, and creamy flavors in a delicious and hearty wrap, perfect for lunch, dinner, or even a quick snack!
