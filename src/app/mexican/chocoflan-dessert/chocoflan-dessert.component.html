Chocotlán

Chocotlán is a traditional Mexican dessert made from a combination of chocolate, nuts, and sometimes dried fruits. This rich and flavorful cake is perfect for special occasions or as a delicious treat. Here’s how to make it Mexican-style.

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup hot water

For the Filling and Topping:

  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (toasted, if preferred)
  • 1/4 cup dried fruit (such as raisins, cranberries, or chopped dried apricots)
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips or chopped chocolate

For the Ganache (Optional):

  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup chocolate chips or chopped chocolate

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Cake Batter

  1. Preheat oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan or line it with parchment paper.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, sift together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Combine wet ingredients: In another bowl, whisk together the vegetable oil, eggs, buttermilk, and vanilla extract.
  4. Mix the batter: Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined. Stir in the hot water; the batter will be thin.
  5. Add mix-ins: Fold in the chopped nuts, dried fruit, and chocolate chips or chunks.

Step 2: Bake the Cake

  1. Pour batter: Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan.
  2. Bake: Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 3: Prepare the Ganache (Optional)

  1. Heat cream: In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it begins to simmer.
  2. Add chocolate: Place the chocolate chips or chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl. Pour the hot cream over the chocolate and let it sit for a few minutes. Stir until smooth and glossy.
  3. Cool: Allow the ganache to cool slightly before spreading it over the cooled cake.

Step 4: Frost and Garnish

  1. Frost the cake: Once the cake has cooled completely, spread the ganache over the top and sides of the cake, if using.
  2. Garnish: Garnish with additional chopped nuts, chocolate shavings, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar, if desired.

Tips for Success

  • Mix-ins: Customize the cake with your favorite nuts, dried fruits, or even chopped candy for added texture and flavor.
  • Ganache: The ganache is optional but adds a rich and decadent touch. If you prefer a simpler cake, dust it with powdered sugar or cocoa powder instead.
