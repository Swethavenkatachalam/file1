Andhra Crab Meat Masala

Andhra Crab Meat Masala is a spicy, flavorful dish from Andhra Pradesh, known for its bold use of spices and fiery taste. This dish highlights the delicate flavor of crab meat, complemented by the region’s signature spice mix. Here’s how to prepare it:

Ingredients

For the Crab Meat:

  • 500g crab meat (cleaned, or whole crabs if preferred)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Masala:

  • 2 tbsp oil (preferably coconut or mustard oil)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Fresh curry leaves (10-12 leaves)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (for a tangy flavor)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Crab Meat
    1. If using whole crabs, clean them thoroughly by removing the shell, legs, and other inedible parts. For just crab meat, clean it properly and set it aside.
    2. Boil the crab meat (or crabs) with turmeric powder, salt, and enough water for about 5-7 minutes until lightly cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Step 2: Make the Masala Base
    1. Heat oil in a large pan or kadhai. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
    2. Add the curry leaves and sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
    3. Add the finely chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
    4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Step 3: Add the Tomatoes and Spices
    1. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook until they turn soft and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
    2. Stir in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, black pepper powder, and salt. Cook the spices on medium heat for 3-4 minutes, allowing them to blend well with the tomatoes and onions.
  4. Step 4: Cook the Crab Meat
    1. Add the pre-cooked crab meat (or crabs) to the masala mixture. Stir gently to coat the crab meat with the spicy masala.
    2. Cover the pan and let it cook on medium heat for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally to ensure the crab meat absorbs the flavors of the masala.
    3. Add garam masala towards the end and mix well.
  5. Step 5: Garnishing and Final Touches
    1. Add lemon juice and fresh coriander leaves to the crab masala for a tangy and fresh finish.
    2. Cook for another 2 minutes, then turn off the heat.
  6. Step 6: Serving
    1. Serve the Andhra Crab Meat Masala hot with steamed rice, roti, or appam.

Tips:

For a spicier flavor, adjust the amount of red chili powder and green chilies.

If you prefer a thicker gravy, reduce the amount of water used during cooking.

Whole crabs give the dish a more authentic touch, but you can use just crab meat for convenience.
