Malai Ghevar

Malai Ghevar is a traditional Indian sweet, especially popular in Rajasthan. Made from refined flour, milk, and soaked in sugar syrup, it's often enjoyed during festivals like Teej and Diwali. The addition of malai (cream) gives it a rich and decadent taste.

Ingredients

For the Ghevar:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/2 cup malai (fresh cream)
  • Oil or ghee (for frying)

For the Sugar Syrup:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/2 tsp lemon juice

For Garnish:

  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios)
  • Rose petals (optional)
  • Edible silver leaf (varak, optional)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Batter
    1. In a large bowl, mix all-purpose flour and semolina.
    2. Gradually add milk and water, stirring continuously to form a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter.
    3. Add baking soda, cardamom powder, and ghee. Mix well and let the batter rest for 30 minutes.
    4. Tip: Ensure there are no lumps in the batter for a smooth texture.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Sugar Syrup
    1. In a pan, combine sugar and water. Heat over medium heat until the sugar dissolves completely.
    2. Add cardamom powder and lemon juice. Cook until the syrup reaches a one-string consistency.
    3. Tip: The lemon juice prevents the syrup from crystallizing.
  3. Step 3: Fry the Ghevar
    1. Heat oil or ghee in a deep frying pan or kadhai. The oil should be moderately hot.
    2. Pour a small amount of batter into the hot oil, and it will spread and form a lace-like pattern. Use a ladle or small cup to pour the batter.
    3. Fry the ghevar until golden brown. Remove it carefully with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
    4. Tip: To get the characteristic lace pattern, pour the batter from a height and ensure the oil isn't too hot.
  4. Step 4: Soak in Sugar Syrup
    1. Once the ghevar is slightly cooled, dip it into the warm sugar syrup for a few seconds to soak.
    2. Remove and let it drain on a wire rack or plate.
    3. Tip: Donâ€™t soak the ghevar for too long, or it may become too soggy.
  5. Step 5: Prepare the Malai
    1. Whip the malai (cream) until smooth and slightly thickened.
    2. Tip: You can add a little sugar to the malai if desired.
  6. Step 6: Assemble and Garnish
    1. Spread a layer of whipped malai on top of the soaked ghevar.
    2. Garnish with chopped nuts, rose petals, and edible silver leaf if using.
    3. Tip: Serve immediately or refrigerate for a few hours for a chilled dessert.

Tips:

- The batter should be smooth and slightly runny. Adjust the milk or water to get the right consistency.

- Maintain a moderate oil temperature for even frying and a lace-like pattern.

- Malai Ghevar is best enjoyed fresh, but it can be refrigerated for a few days. Just add malai and garnish before serving.
