Koliwada Prawns

Koliwada Prawns is a crispy, spicy seafood dish from the Koli community in Mumbai. It's a popular street food, perfect as a snack or appetizer. Serve with lemon wedges and chutney for a tangy kick!

Ingredients

For Marination:

  • 500g medium prawns (cleaned and deveined)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For the Batter:

  • 3 tbsp gram flour (besan)
  • 2 tbsp rice flour (for extra crispiness)
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • A pinch of baking soda (optional, for crispiness)
  • Water (to make a thick batter)

For Frying:

  • Oil for deep frying
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Marinate the Prawns
    1. In a bowl, mix the prawns with ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, ajwain, chaat masala, lemon juice, and salt.
    2. Massage the spices into the prawns. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Batter
    1. In another bowl, combine gram flour, rice flour, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and a pinch of baking soda.
    2. Gradually add water to form a thick batter. The consistency should coat the prawns well but not be too runny.
  3. Step 3: Fry the Prawns
    1. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai over medium heat.
    2. Dip each marinated prawn into the batter, coating it well.
    3. Fry the prawns in batches until golden and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per batch.
    4. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  4. Step 4: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Sprinkle chaat masala on the fried prawns.
    2. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve with lemon wedges.

Tips:

- For a spicier version, increase the red chili powder.

- Add chopped onions, garlic, or ginger to the batter for an extra burst of flavor.

- Serve with mint chutney or as a snack with your favorite beverage.
