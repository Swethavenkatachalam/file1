Chicken Kolhapuri

Chicken Kolhapuri is a fiery and flavorful dish from the Kolhapur region of Maharashtra, India. Made with a blend of aromatic spices, this dish is perfect for spice lovers. Serve with rice or your favorite Indian bread.

Ingredients

For the Kolhapuri Masala (Spice Mix):

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 4-5 dried red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp poppy seeds
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut (fresh or dried)
  • 2 cloves
  • 2 green cardamom pods
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 6-8 black peppercorns

For the Chicken Gravy:

  • 500g chicken (bone-in or boneless, cut into medium pieces)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Kolhapuri Masala
    1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan and roast the coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fennel seeds, dried red chilies, poppy seeds, sesame seeds, grated coconut, cloves, green cardamom, black cardamom, cinnamon, and black peppercorns.
    2. Roast on low-medium heat until the spices turn fragrant and the coconut becomes light golden (about 5-7 minutes).
    3. Allow the mixture to cool, then grind it into a fine powder. This is your Kolhapuri Masala. Set it aside.
  2. Step 2: Cook the Chicken
    1. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large pan or kadhai. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    2. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies, and cook until the raw smell disappears.
    3. Add the chicken pieces and sauté for about 5-7 minutes until they are lightly browned.
    4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Step 3: Make the Gravy
    1. Add the tomato puree to the pan and cook until the oil starts to separate (about 5-6 minutes).
    2. Now, add the freshly ground Kolhapuri Masala to the chicken and stir well. Cook for about 3-4 minutes to let the flavors blend.
    3. Add about 1 cup of water (adjust for desired gravy consistency), cover, and let the chicken simmer on medium heat for 15-20 minutes, or until fully cooked.
    4. Stir in the garam masala, and cook for another 2 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with rice, roti, naan, or bhakri.

Tips:

- For a spicier version, increase the amount of red chili powder.

- Add a tablespoon of tamarind paste for a tangy flavor.

- For a richer gravy, add a tablespoon of ghee before serving.
