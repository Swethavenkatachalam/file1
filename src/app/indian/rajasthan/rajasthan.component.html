Rajasthani Laal Maas

Rajasthani Laal Maas is a fiery, rich mutton curry from Rajasthan, known for its bold red color and spicy kick. It's a traditional dish served during special occasions, and here's how you can make it at home with authentic flavors.

Ingredients

For Marination:

  • 500g mutton (bone-in, cut into medium pieces)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Laal Maas Gravy:

  • 4-5 dried Kashmiri red chilies (soaked in warm water)
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil (or ghee)
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (optional, for extra heat)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Marinate the Mutton
    1. In a bowl, marinate the mutton pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, and salt.
    2. Mix well and let the mutton marinate for at least 1-2 hours (or overnight for better flavor).
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Red Chili Paste
    1. Soak the Kashmiri red chilies in warm water for 15-20 minutes until soft.
    2. Drain the water and blend the chilies into a smooth paste. This gives the dish its signature red color without making it overly spicy. Set aside.
  3. Step 3: Cooking the Laal Maas
    1. Heat mustard oil (or ghee) in a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker until it starts to smoke. Lower the heat and let it cool slightly.
    2. Add cumin seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, and cinnamon. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
    3. Add the chopped onions and green chilies. Cook on medium heat until the onions turn golden brown.
    4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
    5. Now, add the red chili paste and sauté for 3-4 minutes until the oil starts to separate.
    6. Add coriander powder, red chili powder (optional for extra heat), and salt. Mix well.
  4. Step 4: Cooking the Mutton
    1. Add the marinated mutton pieces to the pot and mix them with the masala. Cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mutton is browned and coated well with the spices.
    2. Add about 2 cups of water, cover, and cook the mutton on low heat for 45-60 minutes until it is tender. Alternatively, if using a pressure cooker, cook for 5-6 whistles on medium heat.
    3. Once the mutton is cooked, stir in garam masala and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  5. Step 5: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Garnish the Laal Maas with fresh coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with steamed rice, bajra roti, or naan.

Tips:

- For an even spicier version, add more red chili powder or use Kashmiri red chili powder.

- You can add a tablespoon of tamarind paste for a tangy flavor.

- For a richer gravy, add a tablespoon of ghee to the pot before adding the yogurt and tomato.
