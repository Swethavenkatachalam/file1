Galouti Kebab

Galouti Kebab is a melt-in-your-mouth delicacy from Awadhi cuisine, known for its rich spices and tender texture. Traditionally made with minced mutton, these kebabs are aromatic, flavorful, and perfect for an authentic Indian dining experience.

Ingredients

For the Kebab:

  • 500g minced mutton (finely ground)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp raw papaya paste (tenderizer)
  • 1/4 cup roasted gram flour (besan)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp green cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp mace powder
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg powder
  • 1/2 tsp saffron (soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
  • 2 tbsp fried onions (crushed)
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (finely chopped)

For Cooking:

  • Ghee (for shallow frying)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Minced Mutton
    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine minced mutton with ginger-garlic paste, raw papaya paste, and salt. Mix thoroughly.
    2. Let the mixture marinate for at least 1-2 hours, or overnight for the best results.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Spice Mixture
    1. Dry roast the gram flour (besan) for 2-3 minutes until it turns light golden and aromatic. Set aside to cool.
    2. Add roasted gram flour, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, black pepper, cardamom powder, mace powder, nutmeg powder, saffron milk, crushed fried onions, and coriander leaves to the marinated mutton.
    3. Add 1 tbsp of ghee and mix thoroughly to form a smooth, well-seasoned mince.
  3. Step 3: Shaping the Kebabs
    1. Divide the mixture into small portions and shape them into flat, round patties (around 2 inches in diameter).
    2. Refrigerate the kebabs for about 30 minutes to firm them up.
  4. Step 4: Cooking the Kebabs
    1. Heat ghee in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
    2. Gently place the kebabs in the pan and shallow fry for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown.
  5. Step 5: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Serve the kebabs hot, garnished with coriander leaves, along with roomali roti, onion rings, and green chutney.

Tips:

- The raw papaya paste helps to tenderize the mutton, giving the kebabs their signature soft texture.

- Handle the kebabs delicately while frying as they are very soft.
