Shahi Tukda Recipe

Shahi Tukda is a decadent Indian dessert made from bread, milk, and sugar, often flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts. It’s a royal treat that’s particularly popular during festivals and special occasions. Here’s a traditional recipe for making *Shahi Tukda*:

Ingredients

  • 6 slices of white bread (preferably a day or two old)
  • 1 cup full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup condensed milk (optional, for extra richness)
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
  • 2 tbsp rose water or kewra water (optional, for aroma)
  • Powdered sugar for garnish
  • Chopped nuts and silver leaf (chandi vark) for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Milk Mixture
    1. In a saucepan, bring 1 cup full-fat milk to a boil over medium heat. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    2. Add 1/2 cup sugar and stir until it dissolves completely.
    3. If using, add 1/4 cup condensed milk to the milk mixture for extra richness.
    4. Stir in 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using). Continue to simmer for another 2-3 minutes.
    5. Add rose water or kewra water (if using) for extra aroma. Mix well and remove from heat.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Bread
    1. Trim the crusts from the bread slices if desired.
    2. Heat 1/4 cup ghee in a pan. Fry the bread slices until they are golden brown and crispy on both sides. Remove and drain excess ghee on paper towels.
  3. Step 3: Assemble the Shahi Tukda
    1. Place the fried bread slices on a serving plate or dish.
    2. Pour the prepared milk mixture over the fried bread slices, ensuring they are well soaked.
    3. Garnish with chopped nuts and, if using, silver leaf.
  4. Step 4: Chill and Serve
    1. Allow the Shahi Tukda to sit for at least 30 minutes to soak in the milk and flavors. It can be served warm or chilled.
    2. Garnish with a dusting of powdered sugar and additional nuts if desired.

Tips

  • Using slightly stale bread helps it absorb the milk mixture better. Fresh bread can become too soggy.
  • Fry the bread slices in ghee for a rich flavor. If you prefer a lighter version, you can use oil.
  • Adjust the sweetness of the milk mixture to your taste. If the mixture is too thick, you can add a little more milk.
  • Allowing the Shahi Tukda to sit for a while helps the bread absorb the milk mixture fully and develop a better texture.
