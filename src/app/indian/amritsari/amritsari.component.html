Amritsari Chicken Masala

Amritsari Chicken Masala is a popular Punjabi dish, known for its rich, spicy, and creamy gravy. This flavorful dish combines the tanginess of tomatoes, the richness of butter, and the perfect blend of spices. Here's how to prepare it:

Ingredients

For Marination:

  • 500g chicken (bone-in or boneless, cut into medium pieces)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (dahi)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For the Masala:

  • 2 tbsp butter or oil (preferably ghee)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste (optional for richer color)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream or malai (optional for creaminess)
  • 2 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Marinate the Chicken
    1. In a large bowl, marinate the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt.
    2. Mix well and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours (overnight is best).
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Masala
    1. Heat butter or ghee in a large pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    2. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Saut√© until onions turn golden brown.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Step 3: Add Tomatoes and Spices
    1. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
    2. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Cook for 3-4 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Cook the Chicken
    1. Add the marinated chicken and cook for 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    2. Add 1 cup water, cover, and simmer on low-medium heat for 20-25 minutes until the chicken is tender.
  5. Step 5: Final Touches
    1. Stir in garam masala and kasuri methi. Add fresh cream or malai if desired for a creamy finish.
    2. Taste and adjust seasonings.
  6. Step 6: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with naan, tandoori roti, or steamed basmati rice.

Tips:

If you don't have Amritsari masala, you can make a blend of red chili powder, turmeric, coriander, cumin, fenugreek, and black pepper.

For a creamier texture, add a tablespoon of cashew paste or coconut milk.

To make this vegetarian, substitute chicken with paneer or vegetables.
