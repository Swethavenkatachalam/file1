Jackfruit Biryani

Jackfruit Biryani is a delightful vegetarian twist to the traditional biryani. The meaty texture of young, unripe jackfruit combined with fragrant basmati rice and aromatic spices makes this dish a satisfying alternative for biryani lovers.

Ingredients

For Jackfruit Marination:

  • 500g young, unripe jackfruit (canned or fresh, cut into cubes)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves (a handful)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (a handful)

For Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 4-5 cups water
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cloves
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp ghee

For Layering:

  • Saffron strands (a pinch, soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk)
  • 1/4 cup fried onions
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Mint and coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Preparing the Jackfruit
    1. If using fresh jackfruit, boil it in salted water for 15-20 minutes until tender, then drain and set aside.
    2. In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, green chilies, lemon juice, salt, mint, and coriander leaves.
    3. Add the boiled jackfruit pieces and coat them well with the marinade. Marinate for 1-2 hours (or overnight for deeper flavors).
  2. Step 2: Cooking the Rice
    1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil with cardamom, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, and salt.
    2. Add the soaked basmati rice and cook it until it is 70% done (parboiled). The grains should still be firm.
    3. Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  3. Step 3: Cooking the Marinated Jackfruit
    1. In a deep pan, heat 2 tbsp of ghee. Add the marinated jackfruit and cook on medium heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the jackfruit is slightly browned and aromatic.
    2. Set aside the cooked jackfruit.
  4. Step 4: Layering the Biryani
    1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, add a little ghee and spread a layer of the cooked jackfruit mixture at the bottom.
    2. Add half of the parboiled rice over the jackfruit layer.
    3. Sprinkle some fried onions, saffron milk, mint, and coriander leaves on top.
    4. Add the remaining rice and repeat the layering process with fried onions, saffron milk, mint, and coriander leaves.
  5. Step 5: Dum Cooking (Sealing & Slow Cooking)
    1. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with dough to prevent the steam from escaping.
    2. Cook the biryani on low heat (dum) for 25-30 minutes.
    3. Once done, remove the lid and gently fluff the rice.
  6. Step 6: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Garnish with additional fried onions, fresh mint, and coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with raita, papad, or a side salad.

Tips:

- For a spicier biryani, add more red chili powder to the marination.

- You can add other vegetables like potatoes, carrots, or peas for added flavor and texture.

- For a richer flavor, add a splash of kewra water to the rice.
