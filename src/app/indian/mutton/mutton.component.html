Mutton Kofta

Mutton Kofta is a rich and delicious dish where minced mutton is shaped into meatballs and cooked in a spiced gravy. The dish is popular in Indian cuisine and can be served with rice, naan, or roti. Here's an Indian-style Mutton Kofta recipe with some helpful tips to get it perfect.

Ingredients

For the Koftas (Meatballs):

  • 500g minced mutton (finely ground, with a little fat for softness)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander leaves (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/4 tsp cumin powder
  • 2 tbsp roasted gram flour (besan)
  • 1 egg (optional, for binding)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil (for shallow frying)

For the Gravy:

  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (whisked)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Koftas
    1. In a large bowl, combine minced mutton, onions, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, coriander leaves, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, roasted gram flour, and salt.
    2. If the mixture feels too loose, add an egg for binding or more gram flour as needed.
    3. Shape the mixture into small meatballs (about 1 inch in diameter).
    4. Heat oil in a pan and shallow fry the koftas until golden brown on all sides. Set aside.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Gravy
    1. Heat 2 tbsp oil or ghee in a large pan. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf, and let them splutter.
    2. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
    3. Add tomato puree, turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala.
    4. Lower the heat and stir in whisked yogurt. Cook until the gravy thickens.
  3. Step 3: Simmer and Add Koftas
    1. Add 1/2 cup water to the gravy to adjust consistency. Simmer for 5-7 minutes.
    2. Gently place the fried koftas into the gravy. Simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes, allowing the koftas to absorb the flavors.
    3. Add garam masala, black pepper powder, and crushed kasuri methi. Simmer for 2 more minutes.
  4. Step 4: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Tips:

  • Use finely ground mutton with a bit of fat for tender koftas.
  • If the kofta mixture feels loose, add more gram flour or an egg for better binding.
  • To prevent koftas from breaking, fry on medium heat and avoid crowding the pan.
  • For a richer flavor, cook the onions well and allow the oil to separate from the masala before adding yogurt.
