Surmai Fish Biryani

Surmai Fish Biryani (Kingfish Biryani) is a flavorful and aromatic coastal Indian dish made using Surmai (Kingfish/Seer fish), fragrant basmati rice, and a blend of spices. Follow this recipe to prepare this delicious dish at home!

Ingredients

For Marination:

  • 500g Surmai (Kingfish) steaks, cut into medium pieces
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 4-5 cups water
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 1 tsp shahi jeera (caraway seeds)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp ghee

For Fish Masala:

  • 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk (optional, for coastal flavor)
  • 2 tbsp oil

For Layering:

  • A pinch of saffron strands, soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup fried onions (for garnish)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Mint and coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Marinate the Fish
    1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt.
    2. Coat the Surmai fish pieces with this marinade. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Step 2: Cook the Rice
    1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add cardamom, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, shahi jeera, and salt.
    2. Add the soaked basmati rice and cook until 70% done (parboiled). The grains should be firm.
    3. Drain the rice and set it aside.
  3. Step 3: Prepare the Fish Masala
    1. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    2. Add the sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook until the raw smell disappears.
    4. Add chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes turn soft and the masala becomes thick.
    5. Stir in the coconut milk (optional) for added richness.
    6. Add the marinated fish pieces and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, ensuring not to overcook the fish.
    7. Once done, turn off the heat and add fresh mint and coriander leaves.
  4. Step 4: Layering the Biryani
    1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of the cooked fish masala at the bottom.
    2. Add half of the parboiled rice on top of the fish.
    3. Sprinkle fried onions, saffron milk, mint, and coriander leaves.
    4. Add the remaining rice, followed by another layer of fried onions, saffron milk, mint, and coriander.
  5. Step 5: Dum Cooking (Sealing & Slow Cooking)
    1. Drizzle 2 tbsp ghee over the rice.
    2. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with dough to trap the steam.
    3. Cook the biryani on very low heat for 20-25 minutes. You can place a tawa (flat griddle) under the pot to prevent direct heat.
    4. After 25 minutes, turn off the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before opening the lid.
  6. Step 6: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork.
    2. Garnish with fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves.
    3. Serve hot with raita, papad, and a lemon wedge.

Tips:

- For a spicier biryani, add more red chili powder to the marination.

- You can add other seafood like prawns or squid to the biryani for a variety of flavors.

- For a richer flavor, add a splash of kewra water to the rice.
