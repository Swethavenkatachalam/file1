Seviyan Kheer Recipe

Seviyan Kheer is a rich and comforting Indian dessert made with vermicelli cooked in milk and flavored with cardamom, nuts, and saffron. Here's a detailed recipe to make this delicious treat.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup seviyan (vermicelli)
  • 4 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup condensed milk (optional, for extra richness)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 8-10 almonds (slivered)
  • 8-10 cashews (chopped)
  • 8-10 pistachios (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 8-10 raisins
  • A few strands of saffron (optional, soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
  • A few drops of rose water or kewra water (optional)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Roast the Vermicelli
    1. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat 1 tablespoon of ghee over medium heat.
    2. Add the seviyan (vermicelli) to the hot ghee.
    3. Roast the seviyan, stirring continuously, until it turns golden brown. This should take about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Step 2: Prepare the Milk
    1. In a separate large pan, bring 4 cups of full-fat milk to a boil over medium heat.
    2. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes to slightly thicken. Stir occasionally to prevent the milk from sticking to the bottom.
  3. Step 3: Cook the Seviyan
    1. Add the roasted seviyan to the boiling milk.
    2. Stir well and cook on low to medium heat for about 10-12 minutes, or until the seviyan is soft and has absorbed some of the milk.
    3. Add 1/2 cup sugar and stir until it dissolves completely. If using, add 1/4 cup condensed milk for added richness.
  4. Step 4: Flavor the Kheer
    1. Stir in 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder and the soaked saffron (if using). Mix well.
    2. Continue to cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Step 5: Prepare the Nuts and Raisins
    1. In a small pan, heat a little ghee (about 1 tablespoon) and fry the slivered almonds, chopped cashews, and pistachios until golden brown. Add the raisins and cook for another minute.
    2. Add this mixture to the kheer and stir well.
  6. Step 6: Final Touches
    1. If desired, add a few drops of rose water or kewra water for a subtle floral aroma.
    2. Mix well and let the kheer simmer for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Step 7: Serve
    1. *Seviyan Kheer* can be enjoyed hot, warm, or chilled. If serving chilled, allow it to cool to room temperature before refrigerating.

Tips

  • The kheer will thicken as it cools, so keep it slightly runny if you plan to serve it later. You can adjust the consistency with a little warm milk if needed.
  • Adjust the sugar according to your taste. The kheer may taste sweeter after chilling if youâ€™re serving it cold.
  • For a smoother texture, you can blend a portion of the kheer before adding the nuts. This gives a more uniform consistency.
  • Frying the nuts and raisins in ghee enhances their flavor and adds a nice texture to the kheer. Be cautious not to burn them.
