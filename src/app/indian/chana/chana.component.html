Chana Palak

Chana Palak is a nutritious and delicious Indian dish made with chickpeas (chana) and spinach (palak). It's a wholesome curry that combines the earthy flavors of chickpeas with the vibrant taste of spinach and Indian spices. Here’s how to prepare it:

Ingredients

For the Chana (Chickpeas):

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 1.5 cups canned chickpeas)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

For the Spinach (Palak):

  • 4 cups spinach leaves (washed and chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 medium tomato (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp amchur powder (dry mango powder) or a squeeze of lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Chickpeas
    1. If using dried chickpeas, soak them overnight in water. Drain and rinse them.
    2. Pressure cook the chickpeas with a bay leaf, salt, and enough water for about 6-8 whistles until soft. If using canned chickpeas, drain and rinse them.
    3. Set the cooked chickpeas aside.
  2. Step 2: Blanch and Puree the Spinach
    1. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the chopped spinach leaves and blanch them for 1-2 minutes until wilted.
    2. Drain the spinach and transfer it to a bowl of ice-cold water to retain its green color.
    3. Blend the spinach into a smooth puree using a blender or food processor. Set aside.
  3. Step 3: Make the Masala Base
    1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan or kadhai. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
    2. Add the finely chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
    4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
    5. Stir in the coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to let the spices blend well.
  4. Step 4: Combine the Spinach and Chickpeas
    1. Add the spinach puree to the masala and stir well. Cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing the spinach to absorb the flavors of the masala.
    2. Now, add the cooked chickpeas to the spinach mixture. Mix everything well.
    3. Pour in some water (about 1/2 cup or as needed) to adjust the consistency of the curry.
    4. Simmer the curry for 10-15 minutes on medium heat to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Step 5: Final Touches
    1. Add garam masala and amchur powder (or lemon juice) for a tangy finish. Stir well.
    2. Let the curry simmer for another 2 minutes.
  6. Step 6: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Garnish the Chana Palak with fresh coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with roti, paratha, or steamed rice.
