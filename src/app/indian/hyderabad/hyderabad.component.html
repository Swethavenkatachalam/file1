Hyderabadi Biryani

Hyderabadi Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic dish made with basmati rice, spices, and marinated meat. This authentic recipe, perfect for 4-5 people, captures the essence of traditional Indian cooking with rich spices and herbs.

Ingredients

For Marination:

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into medium pieces)
  • 1 cup thick yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 4-5 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup fried onions (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves (a handful)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (a handful)

For Rice:

  • 3 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 6-7 cups water
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cloves
  • Salt (as required)
  • 1 tbsp ghee

For Layering:

  • Saffron strands (a pinch, soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk)
  • 1/4 cup fried onions
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Mint and coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 2 boiled eggs (optional, for garnishing)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Marinating the Chicken
    1. In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, green chilies, lemon juice, fried onions, salt, mint, and coriander leaves.
    2. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well in this mixture.
    3. Cover the bowl and marinate the chicken for at least 1-2 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator for the best results.
  2. Step 2: Cooking the Rice
    1. In a large pot, boil water with cardamom, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, and salt.
    2. Once the water is boiling, add the soaked basmati rice and cook it until it is about 70% cooked (parboiled). The grains should be firm.
    3. Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  3. Step 3: Layering the Biryani
    1. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat 2 tbsp of ghee. Place the marinated chicken at the bottom in a single layer.
    2. Spread half of the parboiled rice over the chicken.
    3. Sprinkle fried onions, saffron milk, mint leaves, and coriander leaves on top.
    4. Add the remaining rice and repeat the layering with fried onions, saffron milk, mint, and coriander.
  4. Step 4: Dum Cooking (Sealing & Slow Cooking)
    1. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with dough to prevent steam from escaping.
    2. Cook the biryani on low heat (dum) for about 30-40 minutes.
    3. After 40 minutes, remove the lid and gently fluff the rice with a fork.
  5. Step 5: Garnishing and Serving
    1. Garnish with boiled eggs, mint leaves, and coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with raita, mirchi ka salan, or a simple salad.

Tips:

- For a spicier biryani, add more red chili powder to the marination.

- You can substitute lamb or mutton with chicken if preferred.

- For a vegetarian option, use paneer or vegetables instead of meat.
